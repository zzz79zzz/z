Pharmacologic Category
  • Antibiotic, Ophthalmic
Dosing: Adult
Ophthalmic infections/conditions

Ophthalmic infections/conditions: Ophthalmic:

Drops:Instill 1 to 2 drops in affected eye(s) 3 to 4 times daily; to obtain rapid relief in acute conditions, instill 1 to 2 drops every 1 to 2 hours (generally for the first 2 to 3 days).

Ointment: Apply 2 to 3 applications in affected eye(s) daily; for continued effect if drops are used during the day, apply at bedtime.

Dosing: Kidney Impairment: Adult

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling.

Dosing: Hepatic Impairment: Adult

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling.

Dosing: Older Adult

Refer to adult dosing.

Adverse Reactions

The following adverse drug reactions and incidences are derived from product labeling unless otherwise specified. Frequency not defined.

Cardiovascular (with high doses): Cardiac arrhythmia, increased blood pressure, palpitations, tachycardia

Central nervous system (with high doses): Central nervous system depression, dizziness, drowsiness, headache, insomnia, nervousness

Gastrointestinal: Dry mucous membranes

Miscellaneous: Burn

Ophthalmic: Blurred vision

Respiratory: Rhinorrhea, sneezing, stinging sensation of the nose

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to framycetin, other aminoglycosides antibiotics, or any component of the formulation

Because of similarities in chemical structure and/or pharmacologic actions, the possibility of cross-sensitivity may exist with other Streptomyces-derived antibiotics (eg, neomycin, paromomycin, kanamycin)

Warnings/Precautions

Concerns related to adverse effects:

• Superinfection: Prolonged use may result in fungal or bacterial superinfection.

Special populations:

• Contact lens wearers: Formulation contains benzalkonium chloride, which may be absorbed by soft contact lenses; contact lenses should not be worn during treatment of ophthalmologic infections.

Product Availability

Not available in the US

Generic Equivalent Available: US

Yes

Administration: Adult

Ophthalmic: Wash hands prior to administration. For topical ophthalmic use only. Avoid touching tip of applicator to eye or other surfaces.

Use: Labeled Indications

Note: Not approved in the United States.

Ophthalmic infection/conditions: Treatment of ophthalmic infections caused by susceptible bacteria (eg, Staphylococcus aureus, Proteus spp., Pseudomonas aeruginosa, coliforms); treatment of blepharitis, conjunctivitis, corneal ulcers, abrasions, burns, and styes; prophylaxis following removal of foreign bodies.

Metabolism/Transport Effects

None known.

Drug Interactions

There are no known significant interactions.

Monitoring Parameters

Improvement of infection/symptoms

Mechanism of Action

Broad spectrum aminoglycoside antibiotic that is usually bactericidal; appears to inhibit protein synthesis in susceptible bacteria by binding ribosomal subunits. Active against many aerobic gram-negative organisms and some aerobic gram-positive bacteria.
