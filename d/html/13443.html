Notes
  • The OI is used to assess the severity of hypoxic respiratory failure in neonates, infants, children, and adults. In neonates, these indices are most commonly used in patients with PPHN or CDH. In pediatric and adult patients, the OI is sometimes used to assess the severity of ARDS. Higher OI values correlate with increased risk of mortality.
  • In pediatric patients, an OI <8 is an indicator that it may be appropriate to perform extubation readiness testing.
  • In neonatal and pediatric patients, an OI ≥40 is an indicator that it may be appropriate to transfer to an ECMO center.
  • OI : oxygenation index; PPHN: persistent pulmonary hypertension of the newborn; CDH: congenital diaphragmatic hernia; ARDS: acute respiratory distress syndrome; ECMO: extracorporeal membrane oxygenation.


Equations used

OI = FIO 2 * Mean_airway_pressure / PaO 2
