ANSWER — Incorrect.

A therapeutic trial of metoclopramide is appropriate in a patient with diabetes and suspected gastroparesis who has typical clinical features, in whom intestinal obstruction has been ruled out and other common causes of upper gastrointestinal symptoms have been considered, and in whom other features of diabetic microvascular disease are present. However, the dose selected is excessive for a patient with kidney injury. Typical side effects of metoclopramide include drowsiness, restlessness, dystonia, tardive dyskinesia, and Parkinson syndrome.

In patients with normal kidney function, one would ordinarily start with a metoclopramide dose of 5 mg by mouth four times a day (before meals and at bedtime). The patient under consideration has kidney injury. His estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) based on his serum creatinine level, height, and weight is 28.5 mL/minute. At an eGFR of <40 mL/min/1.73 m 2 , 50 percent of the usual metoclopramide dose should be used.
