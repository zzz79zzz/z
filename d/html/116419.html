qSOFA score points






qSOFA interpretation


2 to 3 points: High risk
0 to 1 point: Not high risk




Notes
  • *A Glasgow coma scale score of 15 requires all the following: Spontaneous eye opening; accurate orientation to person, place, and time; and appropriate motor responses to commands.
  • For this calculation, Respiratory rate derived points = 1 when the respiratory rate is ≥22 breaths/min, and 0 for slower rates. Systolic blood pressure derived points = 1 when the systolic blood pressure is ≤100 mmHg, and 0 for higher blood pressures.


Equations used

qSOFA_score = Glasgow_coma_score_derived_points + Respiratory_rate_derived_points + Systolic_blood_pressure_derived_points




Equation parameters, such as Glasgow coma score derived points , have 2 or more discrete values that may be used in the calculation. The numbers in the parentheses, eg, (0), represent the values that will be used.
