Notes
  • In the setting of hypoalbuminemia or hyperalbuminemia, most experts measure the serum ionized calcium. If a laboratory known to measure ionized calcium reliably is not available, the total calcium should be corrected for any abnormality in serum albumin using a calcium correction formula. The accuracy of the corrected calcium is poor in a variety of populations, including patients hospitalized with critical illness and patients with advanced-stage chronic kidney disease.
  • For more information on estimating serum calcium concentration in the presence of abnormal levels of albumin, please refer to UpToDate topic reviews on hyper- and hypocalcemia in adults and children.
  • Some practitioners prefer 40 g/L and others 44 g/L as the Normal albumin. [1]
  • Mean albumin concentration is lower than 40 g/L in infants and children age <1 year. [2]
  • * This calculator uses SI units (Système international d'unités) as the default for inputs. Do not use this calculator if you are using conventional units (also known as US units and gravimetric units); please refer instead to the conventional units version of this calculator.


Equations used

Calcium = Serum_calcium + 0.02 * (Normal_albumin - Patient_albumin)
