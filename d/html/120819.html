What are some things I need to know or do while I take this drug?
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697633 • No Tell all of your health care providers that you take this drug. This includes your doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and dentists.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7089758 • No Have your blood work checked, eye exams, and an ECG (to see how your heart beats) as you have been told by your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7280668 • No Your doctor may order an MRI scan before you start this drug.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5150273 • No If you have not had chickenpox or a chickenpox vaccine, talk with your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6794835 • No Slow or abnormal heartbeat may happen with this drug, especially after your first dose. People with certain heart problems will need to be watched closely for some time after the first dose of this drug. Before starting this drug, tell your doctor about all your other drugs and health problems. Call your doctor right away if you feel confused or have dizziness, slow or abnormal heartbeat, tiredness, or chest pain.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4794383 • No You may have more of a chance of getting an infection. Wash hands often. Stay away from people with infections, colds, or flu. Some infections have been very bad and even deadly.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6727966 • No Talk with your doctor before getting any vaccines while using this drug and for at least 4 weeks after stopping it. Use with this drug may either raise the chance of an infection or make the vaccine not work as well.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6037346 • No High blood pressure has happened with this drug. Have your blood pressure checked as you have been told by your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6794838 • No When this drug is stopped, MS signs can come back and may be worse than before or during treatment. If you stop this drug and your MS signs get worse, call your doctor right away.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5144781 • No Do not stop taking this drug without calling the doctor who ordered it for you.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7067465 • No Certain types of skin cancer have happened with this drug. Avoid lots of sun, sunlamps, and tanning beds. Use sunscreen and wear clothing that protects you from the sun. Have your skin checked as you have been told by your doctor. Call your doctor right away if you have a change in color or size of a mole, new darkened area on your skin, sore that does not heal, or any new or changing skin lump or growth.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7372135 • No Rarely, blood vessel problems have been reported in people taking this drug. Sometimes these problems, like strokes, blood clots in the lungs, and heart attack, can be deadly. Call your doctor right away if you have chest, arm, back, neck, or jaw pain or pressure; coughing up blood; numbness or weakness on 1 side of your body, trouble speaking or thinking, change in balance, or change in eyesight; shortness of breath; or swelling, warmth, or pain in the leg or arm.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4194398 • Female No This drug may cause harm to the unborn baby if you take it while you are pregnant.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6810494 • No If you may become pregnant, you must use birth control while taking this drug and for some time after the last dose. Ask your doctor how long to use birth control. If you get pregnant, call your doctor right away.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697636 • Female No Tell your doctor if you are breast-feeding. You will need to talk about any risks to your baby.
