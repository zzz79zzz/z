Adverse Reactions

The following adverse drug reactions and incidences are derived from product labeling unless otherwise specified.

1% to 10%:

Central nervous system: Confusion, drowsiness

Gastrointestinal: Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting

Respiratory: Hyperventilation

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

• Self-medication (OTC use): Do not use on wounds, damaged skin, broken skin, or irritated skin. Stop use and call health care provider if irritation occurs or if conditions worsen, persist >7 days, or clears up then worsens again in a few days.

Administration: Adult

Topical: Avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes; do not apply to wounds, damaged skin, broken skin, or irritated skin. Massage into painful area until thoroughly absorbed. Do not bandage tightly or apply local heat (eg, heating pad) to the area being treated.

Administration: Pediatric

Topical: For external use only; avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes. Do not apply to wounds or damaged skin. Do not bandage tightly or use with a heating pad.

Use: Labeled Indications

Muscle/Joint pain: Temporary relief of minor aches and pains of muscles and joints associated with arthritis, simple backache, bruises, cramps, muscle strains, and sprains.

Medication Safety Issues
Sound-alike/look-alike issues:

Myoflex may be confused with Mycelex

International issues:

Biafine: Brand name for trolamine [multiple international markets], but also the brand name for a topical emulsion [U.S.]

Metabolism/Transport Effects

None known.

Warfarin: Salicylates (Topical) may enhance the anticoagulant effect of Warfarin. Risk C: Monitor therapy

Pregnancy Considerations

Systemic absorption of salicylate occurs following topical administration of trolamine (Rabinowitz, 1984).
