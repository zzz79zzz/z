Notes
  • The normal urine albumin excretion rate is less than 30 mg/day.
  • Rates between 30 and 299 mg/day are called "moderately increased albuminuria," which was formerly called "microalbuminuria."
  • Rates of 300 mg/day or higher are called "severely increased albuminuria," which was formerly called "macroalbuminuria."
  • The estimated creatinine excretion rate has not been validated in a pediatric population (age <19 years).

 
Equations used
 
Estimated_albumin_excretion_rate = (Spot_urine_albumin / Spot_urine_creatinine) * Estimated_creatinine_excretion_rate

  • For non-black males: Estimated creatinine excretion rate = 1307.3 + (23.1 * Age) - (0.3 * Age2)
