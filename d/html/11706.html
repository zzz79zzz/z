What are some things I need to know or do while I take this drug?
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697633 • No Tell all of your health care providers that you take this drug. This includes your doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and dentists.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6831889 • No Low blood cell counts have happened with this drug. If blood cell counts get very low, this can lead to bleeding problems, infections, or anemia. Sometimes, these have been deadly. If you have questions, talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2698315 • No You may have more chance of getting an infection. Wash hands often. Stay away from people with infections, colds, or flu.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697795 • No You may bleed more easily. Be careful and avoid injury. Use a soft toothbrush and an electric razor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697613 • No Talk with your doctor before getting any vaccines. Use of some vaccines with this drug may either raise the chance of an infection or make the vaccine not work as well.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4471724 • No Patients with cancer who take this drug may be at a greater risk of getting a severe health problem called tumor lysis syndrome (TLS). This may lead to death. Call your doctor right away if you have a fast or abnormal heartbeat; any passing out; trouble passing urine; muscle weakness or cramps; upset stomach, throwing up, diarrhea, or not able to eat; or feel sluggish.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4645516 • No Severe and sometimes deadly liver problems have happened with this drug. Call your doctor right away if you have signs of liver problems like dark urine, tiredness, decreased appetite, upset stomach or stomach pain, light-colored stools, throwing up, or yellow skin or eyes.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5383162 • No Very bad and sometimes deadly bowel problems have happened with this drug. Most of the time, this happened within 30 days of treatment and when more than one chemo drug was used. Talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5383182 • No This drug may cause the release of proteins called cytokines. This may lead to some other health problems and organ problems. Sometimes, these may be deadly. Call your doctor right away if you have a fever, fast heartbeat, fast breathing, shortness of breath, very bad dizziness, or passing out.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4521108 • No If you have upset stomach, throwing up, diarrhea, or decreased appetite, talk with your doctor. There may be ways to lower these side effects.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7082935 • No Have your blood work and heart function checked as you have been told by your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6892996 • No This drug may affect fertility. This may lead to not being able to get pregnant or father a child. If this happens, it is not known if fertility will go back to normal.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6817836 • No This drug may cause harm to an unborn baby. A pregnancy test will be done before you start this drug to show that you are NOT pregnant.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7135074 • No If you or your sex partner may become pregnant, you must use birth control while taking this drug and for some time after the last dose. Ask your doctor how long to use birth control. If you or your sex partner gets pregnant, call your doctor right away.
