MASCC risk index score interpretation


21 to 26 points: Patients at low risk for medical complications and those for whom outpatient management with an oral empiric antibacterial regimen may be safe and effective.
0 to 20 points: Patients at high risk for medical complications. High-risk patients require hospital admission for IV antibiotics and often require a prolonged hospitalization.


Notes
  • *Burden of illness refers to the general clinical status of the patient at the time of presentation with neutropenic fever. For additional guidance, please see appropriate UpToDate topic reviews that address risk assessment of patients with neutropenic fever.
  • ** Previous fungal infection refers to a demonstrated fungal infection or empirically treated fungal infection.
