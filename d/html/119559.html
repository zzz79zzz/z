Notes
  • Ideal body weight, as used in this calculator, is derived from an ideal BMI of 25 kg/m2 as defined in reference.[1]
  • Excess body weight is weight greater than the ideal body weight.
  • Excess BMI is the initial (preoperative) BMI that exceeds 25 kg/m2.
  • BMI: body mass index; TBWL: total body weight loss; EBWL: excess body weight loss; EBMIL: excess body mass index loss.

 
Equations used
 
Ideal body weight = 25 * ((Initial (prebariatric surgery) height/100)2)
Initial BMI = Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight / (Initial (prebariatric surgery) height/100)2
Postoperative BMI = Postoperative weight / (Initial (prebariatric surgery) height/100)2
TBWL = Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight - Postoperative weight
%TBWL = 100 * (Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight - Postoperative weight) / Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight
%EBWL = 100 * (Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight - Postoperative weight) / (Initial (prebariatric surgery) weight - Ideal body weight)
%EBMIL = 100 * (Initial BMI- Postoperative BMI) / (Initial BMI - 25)
