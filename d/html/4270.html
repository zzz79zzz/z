ANSWER — Incorrect.

Duloxetine might be considered a reasonable alternative to pregabalin in this patient. However, it has been associated with modest increases in fasting plasma glucose concentrations and glycated hemoglobin (A1C) values in patients with diabetes. Also, while its beneficial effects are noted as early as one week after the start of treatment, pregabalin may work even faster. For these reasons, this consultant prefers pregabalin when a tricyclic agent is not an appropriate first-choice agent.
