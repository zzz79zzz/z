Notes
  • This tool has been validated for White females, Black/African American females, Hispanic females, and Asian and Pacific Islander females in the United States. The tool may underestimate risk in Black females with previous biopsies and Hispanic females born outside the United States. Because data on American Indian/Alaska Native females are limited, their risk estimates are partly based on data for White females and may be inaccurate. Further studies are needed to refine and validate these models.
  • This calculator is not appropriate for females with a history of breast cancer, ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS), lobular carcinoma in situ (LCIS), or previous radiation therapy to the chest for the treatment of Hodgkin lymphoma.
  • It is also not appropriate for females with a mutation in either the BRCA1 or BRCA2 gene or with a genetic syndrome that may be associated with an elevated risk of breast cancer.
  • First-degree relatives are female birth parent, female siblings, and female offspring.
  • The risk codes above (eg, 0 for "NBiop") are based on data for non-Hispanic White females. Some recoding is done for other groups:
    • For Hispanic females, NBiop remains coded as 0 for no biopsies, and NBiop is recoded to 1 for 1 or more biopsies. For the Age1st risk factor in the Hispanic groups, <20 years remains coded as 0, 20 to 29 years is recoded to 1, and 30+ years or nulliparous is recoded to 2.
    • For African American females, AgeMen of 7 to 11 years is grouped with AgeMen of 12 to 13 years.
    • For Asian American females and for US- and foreign-born Hispanic females, an NRels response of none remains coded as 0; for 1 or more first-degree relatives with breast cancer, NRels is recoded to 1.
    • For non-Hispanic White females and Native American including Alaska Native females, average 5-year and lifetime absolute risk calculations use the WhiteAvg cancer incidence data (h1) and competing mortality data (h2). Average risk calculations for all other groups use their own cancer incidence data (h1) and competing mortality data (h2).

 
Equations used
 
LogRR= (NBiop * NB_data[REN_Beta]) + (AgeMen * AM_data[REN_Beta]) + (Age1st * AF_data[REN_Beta]) + (NRels * NR_data[REN_Beta]) + (Age1st * NRels * AFNR_data[REN_Beta]) + HyperPlasia
LogRR50 = LogRR + (NBiop * A50NB_data[REN_Beta])
RRisk = e(LogRR)
RRisk50 = e(LogRR50)

 

 
Calculation details and variables
  • REN: Race-Ethnicity-Nativity codes; T1 is the age at the start of the risk interval (typically current age); T2 is the age at the end of the risk interval; T2 is set to 90 years old for lifetime risk calculation.
  • REN_Beta = REN when REN is ≤6. For REN >6 REN_Beta is set to 6.
  • NumbrIntvl = T2(rounded up) - T1(rounded down)
  • Absolute risk is calculated by looping through the next steps with index jj spanning jj = 1 to jj = NumbrIntvl:
    • if ((NumbrIntvl >1) and (jj >1) and (jj <NumbrIntvl)) IntgrlLngth = 1
    • else if ((NumbrIntvl >1) and (jj = 1)) IntgrlLngth = 1 - (T1 - T1(rounded down))
    • else if ((NumbrIntvl >1) and (jj = NumbrIntvl)) IntgrlLngth = (T2 - T2(rounded down)) * (T2 >T2(rounded down)) + (T2 = T2(rounded down))
    • else if (NumbrIntvl = 1) IntgrlLngth = T2 - T1
    • ARxRR = AR50[age category above or below 50][REN_Beta] * Relative Risk[age category above or below 50]
    • Hj = (h1 * ARxRR) + h2 where h1 and h2 are the AgeGrp-REN coefficients from the h1 and h2 tables below
    • PIj = ((ARxRR * h1 / Hj) * e(-CumulativeH)) * (1 - e(-Hj * IntgrlLngth))
    • CumulativeH = CumulativeH + Hj * IntgrlLngth
    • RskWrk = RskWrk + PIj
  • When the loop is complete, the final risk percentage is: 100 * RskWrk
  • Average risks are calculated by ignoring the patient relative risks, and setting ARxRR to 1 in the loop above.
