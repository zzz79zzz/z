Tinea corporis, tinea pedis

Tinea cruris (spray only): Topical: Apply twice daily to affected area for 2 weeks.

Adverse Reactions

There are no adverse reactions listed in the manufacturer's labeling.

Contraindications

OTC labeling: When used for self-medication, do not use on children <2 years or for diaper rash.

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

• Self-medication (OTC use): When used for self-medication, discontinue use and consult a health care provider if irritation occurs or condition does not improve within 4 weeks. Avoid contact with eyes. Not effective on the scalp or nails.

Administration: Adult

Topical: For topical use only. Apply thin layer to clean, dry skin; not effective on scalp or nails.

Ointment: When using for toe fungus, apply under the nail and around the cuticle.

Tinea pedis: Pay special attention to spaces between the toes; wear well-fitting, ventilated shoes, and change shoes/socks at least once daily.

Administration: Pediatric

Topical: Clean and dry the affected area before topical application

Gel, liquid, ointment, solution, spray: Apply a thin layer over affected area

Powder: Apply sparingly over affected area

Use: Labeled Indications

Tinea corporis: Treatment of ringworm (tinea corporis)

Tinea cruris (spray only): Treatment of jock itch (tinea cruris)

Tinea pedis: Treatment of athlete's foot (tinea pedis)

Metabolism/Transport Effects

None known.

Drug Interactions

There are no known significant interactions.

Mechanism of Action

Fungistatic fatty acid; may include zinc, providing an astringent action, reducing rawness and irritation.
