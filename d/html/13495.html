Notes
  • BUN : blood urea nitrogen; Osm : osmolality.
  • The plasma osmolality is determined by the concentration of the different solutes in the plasma.
  • In normal subjects, sodium salts (chloride and bicarbonate), glucose, and urea are the primary circulating solutes.
  • The calculated plasma osmolality normally is between 270 and 290 mOsm/kg H 2 O and the Osmolal gap is normally <10 mOsm/kg H 2 O. You can calculate the Osmolal gap from the formulas depicted.
  • * This calculator uses SI units (Système international d'unités) as the default for inputs. Do not use this calculator if you are using conventional units (also known as US units and gravimetric units); please refer instead to the conventional units version of this calculator.


Equations used

Calculated_Osm = (Serum_Na * 2) + Serum_glucose + Serum_BUN + (Serum_ethanol * 1.25)
Osmolal_gap = Measured_Osm - Calculated_Osm
