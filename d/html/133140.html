Notes
  • * Patients with grade I encephalopathy may have mild asterixis, whereas pronounced asterixis is seen in patients with grade II or III encephalopathy. Asterixis is typically absent in patients with grade IV encephalopathy, who instead may demonstrate decorticate or decerebrate posturing.[1]
  • For patients with cirrhosis, the CLIF-SOFA score helps predict in-hospital mortality and determine the overall severity of illness (eg, spontaneous bacterial peritonitis).[2] Refer to UpToDate content on treatment of spontaneous bacterial peritonitis.
  • CLIF-SOFA: chronic liver failure-sequential organ failure assessment;[3] HE: hepatic encephalopathy; E: epinephrine; NE: norepinephrine; PaO2: partial pressure (arterial) oxygen; FiO2: fraction inspired oxygen; SpO2: oxygen saturation by pulse oximetry; EEG: electroencephalogram.
