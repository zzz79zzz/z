Calculator: Ranson criteria for pancreatitis prognosis


Criteria present at 0 hours:

Age >55 years old (1 point)

WBC >16,000/microL (1 point)

Glucose >200 mg/dL [>11.1 mmol/L] (1 point)

LDH >350 international units/L [>5.83 microkat/L] (1 point)

AST >250 international units/L [>4.17 microkat/L] (1 point)
Criteria present at 48 hours:

Hematocrit fall of 10% or greater (1 point)

BUN rise of 5 mg/dL [1.8 mmol/L] or more despite fluids (1 point)

Serum calcium <8 mg/dL [<2 mmol/L] (1 point)

pO 2 <60 mmHg [<8 kPa] (1 point)

Base deficit >4 mEq/L [>4 mmol/L] (1 point)

Fluid sequestration >6000 mL (1 point)

Ranson score interpretation


0 to 2 points: Mortality 0 to 3%
3 to 5 points: Mortality 11 to 15%
6 to 11 points: Mortality ≥40%


Notes
  • Mortality increases with an increasing score.
  • Limited information is available regarding the performance of prognostic scoring systems for acute pancreatitis in children, and the clinical utility in children is questionable. [2]
  • Please refer to appropriate UpToDate topic reviews for further information.
  • WBC : white blood cell; LDH : lactate dehydrogenase; AST : alanine aminotransferase; BUN : blood urea nitrogen; pO 2 : partial pressure of oxygen.


References

  2. Perito ER, Lowe ME, Schwarzenberg SJ. Predicting severity in acute pancreatitis in children: What can a quantitative score add to clinical judgement? J Pediatr Gastroenterol Nutr 2020; 71:419.
