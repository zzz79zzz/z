Notes
  • The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists endorses the use of the spot urine protein to creatinine ratio ≥0.3 mg protein/mg creatinine to allow rapid diagnosis of preeclampsia, since the estimated 24-hour urine protein excretion at this level is generally ≥300 mg. [2]
  • A urine protein to creatinine ratio less than 0.15 mg/mg is highly predictive of <300 mg protein in a 24-hour collection, and values above 0.7 mg/mg are highly predictive of >300 mg protein in a 24-hour collection. When time permits and clinically important, ratios between 0.15 and 0.7 mg/mg can be further evaluated by 24-hour urine collection. Please refer to the UpToDate topic on evaluation and management of proteinuria in pregnancy.


Equations used
