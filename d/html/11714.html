What are some things I need to know or do while I take this drug?
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697633 • No Tell all of your health care providers that you take this drug. This includes your doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and dentists.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697613 • No Talk with your doctor before getting any vaccines. Use of some vaccines with this drug may either raise the chance of an infection or make the vaccine not work as well.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2698463 • No Have blood work checked as you have been told by the doctor. Talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5695874 • No The doctor will watch blood pressure, heart rate, and oxygen levels closely. Talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7362663 • No Kidney problems have happened with human immune globulin. Sometimes, this has been deadly. Kidney problems are more common with products that have sucrose. This product has sucrose. The chance may be raised if you already have kidney problems or if you have high blood sugar (diabetes), fluid loss (dehydration), low blood volume, a blood infection, or abnormal proteins in the blood. The chance may also be raised if you are 65 or older, or if you take other drugs that may harm the kidneys. Talk with your doctor if you have questions.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697843 • No This drug is made from human plasma (part of the blood) and may have viruses that may cause disease. This drug is screened, tested, and treated to lower the chance that it carries an infection. Talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697004 • No If you are 65 or older, use this drug with care. You could have more side effects.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6769396 • Female No Tell your doctor if you are pregnant, plan on getting pregnant, or are breast-feeding. You will need to talk about the benefits and risks to you and the baby.

Call your doctor right away if you have a headache, fever, chills, very upset stomach or throwing up, stiff neck, rash, bright lights bother your eyes, feeling sleepy, or feeling confused.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6031194 • No Lung problems have happened with this drug. Call your doctor right away if you have lung or breathing problems like trouble breathing, shortness of breath, or a cough that is new or worse.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4555924 • No There is a chance of blood clots with this drug. The chance is raised in older people and in people with thick blood, heart problems, or a history of blood clots. However, blood clots can happen even if you do not have any of these health problems. Call your doctor right away if you have numbness or weakness on 1 side of your body; pain, redness, tenderness, warmth, or swelling in the arms or legs; change in color of an arm or leg; chest pain or pressure; shortness of breath; fast heartbeat; or coughing up blood. Talk with your doctor.
