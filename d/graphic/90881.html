Histologic features of mycosis fungoides
Mycosis fungoides. Hyperchromatic lymphocytes with irregular nuclear contours and surrounding haloes align along the dermal-epidermal junction and invade the epidermis with minimal associated spongiosis. Focally, the intraepidermal lymphocytes form small clusters called "Pautrier's microabscesses."
