Resources for clinicians and families of gifted children
National organizations
The Association for the Gifted
cectag.com		 A division of the Council of Exceptional Children; dedicated to helping parents and professionals work together successfully.
Davidson Institute for Talent Development
www.davidsongifted.org/		 Focuses on the needs of highly and profoundly gifted young people.
Duke University Talent Identification Program
tip.duke.edu		 Provides information and resources about the identification and support of academically gifted students.
The Gifted Child Society
www.gifted.org/		 Provides educational enrichment and support services, assistance to parents, and professional training.
Hoagie's Gifted Page
www.hoagiesgifted.org		 Provides a variety of information and resources.
National Association for Gifted Children
www.nagc.org		 Nonprofit organization of parents, teachers, and other professionals; provides position papers and local contact information for parents of gifted children. Focus is on schoolhouse giftedness.
National Research Center on the Gifted and Talented
nrcgt.uconn.edu		 Provides research and informational resources.
Supporting the Emotional Needs of the Gifted
sengifted.org		 Focuses on the social and emotional needs of the gifted.
Twice-Exceptional Newsletter
https://www.2enews.com/		 Provides information and resources for parents, teachers, and counselors of gifted children with learning difficulties.

Helping Gifted Children Soar: A Practical Guide for Parents and Teachers, 2nd ed. by Carol Strip Whitney and Gretchen Hirsch (2011)
Keys to Parenting the Gifted Child, 3rd ed. by Sylvia B Rimm (2006)
Parents' Guide to Raising a Gifted Child: Recognizing and Developing Your Child's Potential from Preschool to Adolescence by James Alvino (1996)
Stand Up for Your Gifted Child: How to Make the Most of Kids' Strengths at School and at Home by Joan F Smutny (2000)
The Survival Guide for Parents of Gifted Kids: How to Understand, Live With, and Stick Up for Your Gifted Child by Sally Yahnke Walker (2002)
Perfectionism: What's Bad About Being Too Good by Jan Goldberg and Miriam Elliot (1992)
Why Bright Kids Get Poor Grades: And What You Can Do About It: A Six-Step Program for Parents and Teachers By Sylvia B Rimm (2008)
