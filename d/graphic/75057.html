Erythroid hyperplasia
Bone marrow aspirate from a patient with chronic hemolytic anemia showing markedly increased numbers of red cell precursors (erythroid hyperplasia). The majority of the cells in this field are basophilic or polychromatophilic normoblasts, with varying degrees of darkly staining nuclear chromatin. (Wright-Giemsa stain).
