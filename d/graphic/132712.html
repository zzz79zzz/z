Approach to pregnancies with isolated soft markers

Approach to pregnancies with isolated soft markers
Soft marker Definition Aneuploidy association Counseling and management
Echogenic intracardiac focus Small (<6mm) echogenic area in either cardiac ventricle, as bright as surrounding bone. LR 1.4 to 1.8 for trisomy 21
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening
  • Prior negative screening: no further aneuploidy testing
  • No indication for fetal echocardiogram, follow-up ultrasound, or postnatal evaluation
Echogenic bowel Fetal bowel as bright or brighter than surrounding fetal bone LR 4 to 8 for trisomy 21. Also reported with trisomy 18/13 and monosomy X
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening
  • Prior negative screening: no further aneuploidy testing
  • Evaluation for cystic fibrosis and fetal CMV infection
  • Third trimester growth ultrasound 
Choroid plexus cyst Small fluid-filled structure within the choroid of the lateral ventricles of the fetal brain LR 0.9 to 5.6 for trisomy 18
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening
  • Prior negative screening: no further aneuploidy testing
  • No indication for follow-up ultrasound or postnatal evaluation
Single umbilical artery Umbilical cord with one artery and one vein, identified on cross section of umbilical cord No increased risk for aneuploidy when isolated
  • No additional aneuploidy screening
  • Third trimester ultrasound for fetal growth and consideration of weekly antenatal testing at 36 0/7 weeks
Urinary tract dilation Increased anterior-posterior renal pelvis diameter with normal calyceal dilation, parenchymal thickness, ureteral dilation, bladder, amniotic fluid volume  LR 1.5 for trisomy 21
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening
  • Prior negative screening: no further aneuploidy testing
  • Third trimester follow-up ultrasound to determine if newborn evaluation is indicated
Shortened humerus or femur Ratio of the observed to expected length of <0.90 for the humerus and <0.92 for the femur, based on gestational age by biparietal diameter
  • Shortened humeri: LR 5.1 to 7.5 for trisomy 21
  • Shortened femurs: LR 1.5 to 2.7 for trisomy 21 
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening
  • Prior negative screening: no further aneuploidy testing
  • Thorough evaluation to rule out skeletal dysplasia
  • Third trimester ultrasound to evaluate fetal growth
Thickened nuchal fold Imaged in the transverse plane of the fetal head, to include the cerebellum and the occipital bone. Measurement ≥6 mm between 15-20 weeks  LR 3.8 for trisomy 21
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening or diagnostic testing
  • Prior negative serum screening: option of no further testing, cfDNA screening, or diagnostic testing
  • Prior negative cfDNA screening: no further aneuploidy testing
Absent or hypoplastic nasal bone Imaged perpendicular to the longitudinal axis of the nose, in the midsagittal plane of the fetal face LR 6.6 for trisomy 21
  • No prior screening: offer serum screening or cfDNA screening or diagnostic testing
  • Prior negative serum screening: option of no further testing, cfDNA screening, or diagnostic testing
  • Prior negative cfDNA screening: no further aneuploidy testing
LR: likelihood ratio; CMV: cytomegalovirus.
