Algorithm for evaluation of asymptomatic proteinuria in children
Refer to UpToDate topics on the evaluation of proteinuria in children for further details.
BUN: blood urea nitrogen; C3: complement component 3; C4: complement component 4.
* For children between 6 and 24 months, the threshold value is 0.5 mg protein to mg creatinine.
¶ Persistent proteinuria, elevated blood pressure, and impaired renal function (elevated serum creatinine) are suggestive of renal parenchymal damage. Such patients should be referred to a pediatric nephrologist (or a clinician with expertise in the care of children with renal disease).
