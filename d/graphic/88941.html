Triradiate cartilage in a child's pelvis
The Triradiate cartilage (arrow) typically fuses when a child is between the ages of 14 and 16 years. During childhood the Triradiate cartilage complicates the interpretation of pelvic radiographs. Note that the cartilage is present bilaterally and appears symmetric.
