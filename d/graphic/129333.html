European League Against Rheumatism and American College of Rheumatology provisional criteria for classification of polymyalgia rheumatica
Required criteria: age 50 years or older, bilateral shoulder aching, and abnormal C-reactive protein and/or erythrocyte sedimentation rate
Clinical criteria for scoring algorithm* Points
1. Morning stiffness lasting more than 45 minutes 2
2. Hip pain or restricted range of motion 1
3. Absence of rheumatoid factor and antibody to cyclic citrullinated peptide 2
4. Absence of other joint involvement 1
Ultrasound criteria for scoring algorithm*  
5a. At least one shoulder with subdeltoid bursitis, biceps tenosynovitis, or glenohumeral synovitis; and at least one hip with synovitis or trochanteric bursitis 1
5b. Both shoulders with subdeltoid bursitis, biceps tenosynovitis, or glenohumeral synovitis 1
* With only clinical criteria, a score of ≥4 had a sensitivity of 68% and specificity of 78% for discriminating polymyalgia rheumatica from comparison patients. With a combination of clinical criteria and ultrasound criteria, a score of ≥5 had a sensitivity of 66% and specificity of 81% for discriminating patients with the disorder from comparison patients.
