Heel raise test for spring ligament
A heel raise test is performed to determine if posterior tibialis (PT) function remains intact. As the patient performs a heel raise, the longitudinal arch reforms as the PT muscle contracts, indicating an intact PT tendon. Intact PT function with collapse of the longitudinal arch at rest indicates that the calcaneo-navicular (ie, spring) ligament is not functioning.
