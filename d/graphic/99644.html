Alternative pathway cofactor activity
A cofactor, such as CD46 (shown) or factor H, binds to C3b attached to host cell membranes. This allows serine protease factor I to cleave C3b to prevent further C3 activation. In the classical and lectin pathways, C4b is inactivated by the same mechanism, with CD46 or C4b binding protein serving as the cofactor protein.
