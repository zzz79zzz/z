Schematic depiction of risk factors for atherosclerosis in transplant recipients
The risk factors for cardiovascular disease found in the transplant recipient include hyperlipidemia, hypertension, diabetes mellitus, hyperhomocystenemia, uremia, and others (shaded boxes). Many of these factors are exacerbated by the immunosuppressive drugs (dashed boxes) in current clinical use to prevent rejection.
