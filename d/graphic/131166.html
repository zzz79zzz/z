Stages of tricuspid regurgitation

Stages of tricuspid regurgitation
Stage Definition Valve hemodynamics Hemodynamic consequences Clinical symptoms and presentation
B Progressive TR
  • Central jet <50% RA
  • Vena contracta width <0.7 cm
  • ERO <0.40 cm2
  • Regurgitant volume <45 mL
  • None
  • None
C Asymptomatic severe TR
  • Central jet ≥50% RA
  • Vena contracta width ≥0.7 cm
  • ERO ≥0.40 cm2
  • Regurgitant volume ≥45 mL
  • Dense continuous wave signal with triangular shape
  • Hepatic vein systolic flow reversal
  • Dilated RV and RA
  • Elevated RA with "c-V" wave
  • Elevated venous pressure
  • No symptoms
D Symptomatic severe TR
  • Central jet ≥50% RA
  • Vena contracta width ≥0.7 cm
  • ERO ≥0.40 cm2
  • Regurgitant volume ≥45 mL
  • Dense continuous wave signal with triangular shape
  • Hepatic vein systolic flow reversal
  • Dilated RV and RA
  • Elevated RA with "c-V" wave
  • Elevated venous pressure
  • Dyspnea on exertion, fatigue, ascites, edema
TR: tricuspid regurgitation; RA: right atrial; ERO: effective regurgitant orifice; RV: right ventricular; c-V wave: systolic positive wave.
