Phytophotodermatitis
This burn-like skin lesion occurs when sunlight interacts with photosensitizing compounds found in certain fruits or vegetables (eg, limes, lemons, figs, parsnips, celery). The pattern of lesion corresponds to the contact (eg, streaks from dripping juice, handprints from adults handling the sensitizing material and then the child), or around the hands and mouth after the child handles or ingests the agent.
