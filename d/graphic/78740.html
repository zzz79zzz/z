Hand arthritis in hemochromatosis
Serial radiographs, five years apart, of the metacarpophalangeal joints in a patient with hereditary hemochromatosis (HH). The second image shows loss of joint space at the second and third metacarpophalangeal articulations, cyst formation, and a hook-like osteophyte on the radial aspect of the metacarpal head of the third finger (arrow).
