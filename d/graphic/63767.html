Alendronate dose response in osteoporosis
Mean changes in BMD (measured by DXA) in the lumbar spine and femoral neck in postmenopausal women with osteoporosis treated with placebo or different doses of alendronate for three years. Alendronate caused an increase in bone density, an effect that was most pronounced at a dose of 10 mg/day (red squares). Bone density fell in the placebo group.
BMD: bone mineral density; DXA: dual-energy x-ray absorptiometry.
Data from: Liberman UA, Weiss SR, Broll J, et al. Effect of oral alendronate on bone mineral density and the incidence of fractures in postmenopausal osteoporosis. The Alendronate Phase III Osteoporosis Treatment Study Group. N Engl J Med 1995; 333:1437.
