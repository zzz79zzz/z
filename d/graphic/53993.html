Choledochoduodenal anastomotic stricture

(A) In a patient with a choledochoduodenal anastomosis, injection of contrast material into the bile ducts showed dilated intrahepatic ducts (arrow) with no passage of contrast through the anastomosis. A catheter (arrowhead) was inserted through the bile ducts and into the duodenum.

(B) Using a transhepatic approach, the catheter was inserted through the anastomotic stricture. Next, a balloon was inflated (dashed arrows) to dilate the stricture.
