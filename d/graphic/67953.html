The incidence of aortic regurgitation with dexfenfluramine is associated with diastolic blood pressure
The frequency of any degree of aortic regurgitation (AR) in dexfenfluramine patients increases progressively with higher diastolic blood pressures (p = 0.03); there is no effect of diastolic pressure on the incidence of AR in control subjects.
Data from Shively BK, Roldan CA, Gill EA, et al. Circulation 1999; 100:2161.
