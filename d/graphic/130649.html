Dermoscopic characteristics of skin lesions of lipoid proteinosis
(A) Left axilla showing pale white, structureless areas (triangles) and sulci and gyri (arrows).
(B) Nape of the neck showing round, pinkish-white structures in clumps, giving a "pulpy" or "pulp-like" appearance (circles).
(C) Characteristic eyelid beading (square) and distichiasis of the right upper eyelid (arrowhead). The eyelashes are misaligned.
From: Tabassum H, Mushtaq S, Amin SS, et al. Lipoid Proteinosis with Esotropia: Report of a Rare Case and Dermoscopic Findings. Indian J Dermatol 2020; 65:53. DOI: 10.4103/ijd.IJD_523_18.
