Champion proposed definition of growing pains: Inclusion and exclusion criteria
Essential growing pain criteria
  1. Pain in both legs
  2. Pain started between ages 3 and 12 years
  3. Pain typically occurs at the end of the day or during the night
  4. No significant limitation of activity and no limping
Excluding factors
  1. A pattern of pain severity and persistence not consistent with a diagnosis of growing pains
  2. Any indication of a definite orthopedic or neurologic disorder
  3. Any abnormalities on specific testing (eg, radiographs, bone scans)
  4. The leg pain is associated with a strong urge to move them[1]
Additional descriptive features of growing pains
  1. Pain persists for at least 3 months
  2. There are periods of days, weeks, or months without leg pains
  3. Pain is not a problem in the morning
  4. There is no associated lack of well-being
If at least 3 of 4 essential growing pain characteristics are present in the absence of any excluding factors, the individual is classified as fulfilling criteria for growing pains.
