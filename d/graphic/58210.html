Wandering atrial pacemaker
Electrocardiogram showing prominent beat-to-beat variation in P wave axis or morphology indicative of a wandering (shifting) atrial pacemaker. The P wave is upright in the first and third beats, biphasic in the last beat, and flat or inverted in the other beats. This pattern may occur as a physiologic variant, especially during sleep, or as a manifestation of drug toxicity (digitalis, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers) or the sick sinus syndrome. The relatively slow rate distinguishes this arrhythmia from multifocal atrial tachycardia.
