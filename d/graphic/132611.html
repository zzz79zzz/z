Death rates* from colorectal cancer,¶ by age group, in the United States 1999 to 2019
During 1999 to 2019, deaths per 100,000 persons from colorectal cancer decreased among persons aged 55 to 64 years (from 33.5 to 24.4), persons aged 65 to 74 years (from 77.4 to 41.5), and persons aged 75 to 84 years (from 146.7 to 77.9). The death rate from colorectal cancer among persons aged 45 to 54 years generally increased from 1999 (11.1) to 2019 (12.0). In each year during 1999 to 2019, the death rate was highest among persons aged 75 to 84 years and lowest among persons aged 45 to 54 years.
* Deaths per 100,000 population in each age group.
¶ Deaths from colorectal cancer were identified using International Classification of Diseases, Tenth Revision underlying cause-of-death codes C18-21.
