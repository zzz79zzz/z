Visual decision aid depicting the benefits and risks of endovascular thrombectomy for patients ineligible for IV tPA
Choice consequence matrix type visual decision aid depicting the benefits and risks of endovascular thrombectomy among tPA-ineligible patients. Dark green, attainment of excellent outcome (mRS, 0-1) as a result of thrombectomy; light green, improved disability outcome (other than excellent outcome) as a result of thrombectomy; light red, worse disability outcome (other than severely disabled/dead) as a result of thrombectomy; open rectangle, infarct in new territory as a result of thrombectomy.
tPA: tissue-type plasminogen activator; IV: intravenous; mRS: modified Rankin scale; SICH: symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage.
* None were severely disabled or dead (mRS, 5-6) due to thrombectomy.
¶ No differences observed in the rate of SICH due to thrombectomy.
From: Tokunboh I, Vales Montero M, Zopelaro Almeida MF, et al. Visual aids for patient, family, and physician decision making about endovascular thrombectomy for acute ischemic stroke.
