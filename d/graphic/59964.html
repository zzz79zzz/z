Surgical incisions used for minimally invasive CABG
Conventional CABG uses a long median sternotomy incision (A), while the incisions for minimally invasive CABG are small (5 to 7 cm) and include transternal (B), right parasternal (C) extending over the fourth and fifth costal cartilages, left parasternal (D) extending over the third and fourth costal cartilages, and limited thoracotomy (E).
Adapted from: Stanbridge RD, Hadjinikolaou LK, Cohen AS, et al. Ann Thorc Surg 1997; 65 (6 Suppl):S53.
