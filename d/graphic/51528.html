Aortic dilation in aortic stenosis
Aortic pathology can be better seen by imaging the ascending aorta one interspace above the usual long axis precordial window. The normal appearance of the sinuses and ascending aorta (Asc Ao) is seen in panel A. For comparison, panel B shows poststenotic dilatation which is quite typical and almost always found in aortic stenosis.
AV: aortic valve; LA: left atrium; LV: left ventricle.
