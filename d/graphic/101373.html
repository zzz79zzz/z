Valgus stress test of elbow
With the patient's elbow passively flexed to about 30 degrees and their forearm in neutral position, the examiner applies a valgus stress to the elbow. A soft endpoint or palpable joint widening of 2 mm or more compared to the opposite, unaffected elbow marks a positive test.
