Pacing leads for cardiac resynchronization therapy
Two leads (right atrial and right ventricular leads) permit pacing of the right atrium and right ventricle. The third lead (coronary sinus lead), which is advanced through the coronary sinus into a venous branch that runs along the free wall of the left ventricle, paces the lateral wall and enables synchronized left ventricular contraction.
