Salivary gland involvement in IgG4-related disease and Sjögren's disease
  IgG4-RD Sjögren's disease
Gender distribution 2.5:1 male:female ratio 9:1 female:male ratio
Clinical presentation Mild or no salivary gland dysfunction Significant salivary gland dysfunction (xerostomia)
Persistent salivary gland swelling Intermittent salivary gland swelling
Circulating antibodies Serum IgG4 and IgE levels typically elevated Serum IgG4 and IgE levels usually normal
Anti-Ro/SSA and anti-LA/SSB rarely present Anti-Ro/SSA (with or without anti-LA/SSB) commonly present
ANA detectable in 25% of patients ANA detectable in 90% of patients
Rheumatoid factor detected in 25% of patients Rheumatoid factor detected in 60% of patients
Imaging No abnormality of salivary glands seen on sialography Punctate and globular sialectasis seen on sialography
IgG4-RD: immunoglobulin G4-related disease; IgE: immunoglobulin E; ANA: antinuclear antibodies.
Adapted from: Fragoulis GE, Zampeli E, Moutsopoulos HM. IgG4-related sialadenitis and Sjögren's syndrome. SO Oral Dis 2017; 23:152.
