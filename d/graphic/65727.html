Foamy degeneration in primary biliary cholangitis
Foamy degeneration of hepatocytes adjacent to portal triads in a patient with primary biliary cholangitis. There are hyaline droplets in many of these swollen hepatocytes, which are similar to those seen in alcoholic hepatitis. The lesion is thought to be due to the toxic effect of retained bile acids (Masson trichrome, x496).
