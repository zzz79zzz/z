Intraoperative approach to a hypotensive patient with presumed LV failure*¶
This algorithm is meant to serve as an overview of intraoperative management of LV failure. Details regarding treatment of heart failure, shock, and intraoperative hemodynamic management can be found in separate topics.
LV: left ventricular; FiO2: fraction of inspired oxygen; PEEP: positive end-expiratory pressure; SPV or PPV: systolic pressure variations or pulse pressure variations with respiration noted on the intra-arterial pressure waveform; CVP: central venous pressure; TEE: transesophageal echocardiography; TTE: transthoracic echocardiography; RV: right ventricle; SVR: systemic vascular resistance; ICU: intensive care unit; pRBC: packed red blood cells; IABP: intraaortic balloon pump; LVAD: left ventricular assist device; ECMO: extracorporeal membrane oxygenation.
* Refer to to UpToDate's content on management of shock.
¶ Refer to UpToDate content on management of heart failure.
Δ Refer to UpToDate content regarding dynamic hemodynamic parameters, rescue echocardiography, and focused cardiac ultrasound.
◊ Hypovolemia is likely (ie, inadequately filled LV); evidence may include:
SPV/PPV >10%, CVP <12 mmHg, or TEE or TTE evidence of LV underfilling;
OR
Hypovolemia is unlikely (ie, adequately filled LV); evidence may include:
SPV / PPV <10%, CVP >12 mmHg, or TEE or TTE evidence of adequately filled LV.
§ Refer to UpToDate content regarding vasoactive agents.
¥ Refer to UpToDate content regarding intraoperative fluid management.
‡ Refer to UpToDate content regarding IABP, LVAD, and ECMO.
