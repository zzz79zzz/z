ND-PAE symptom diagnosis guidelines for children 1 through 4 years of age: Neurocognitive, self-regulation, and adaptive domains
Neurocognitive domains Self-regulation domains Adaptive domains
General cognitive impairment:
  • Mild or global developmental delays
  • Problems applying knowledge to new situations
  • Lack of elaborate imaginative play

Executive functioning:

  • Difficulty transitioning from one activity to another
  • Problems learning sequential information – first to last, days of the week, least to most
  • Repeatedly making the same mistakes
  • Has trouble organizing themself
  • Cannot describe events orderly or in a logical sequence
  • Is disorganized
  • Is inflexible
  • Acts without thinking
  • Gets stuck on things
  • Poor understanding of consequences
  • Poor linking of acts to consequences
  • Difficulty thinking about things differently
  • Does not plan to complete complex tasks
  • Lies about things that do not make sense

Learning:

  • Requires repeated exposures to things to learn properly
  • Struggles with learning early academic concepts
  • Struggles with early math concepts (one to one correspondence in counting objects, object constancy)

Memory:

  • Problems remembering something learned recently
  • Cannot hold information in their memory
  • Cannot carryout multiple step plans
  • Has poor recall of information or events
  • Frequently forgets things

Visual-spatial reasoning:

  • Difficulty constructing three-dimensional figures out of blocks
  • Has poor understanding of their body in space
  • Difficult copying simple visual patterns
  • Difficulty with learning spatial words (left, right, up, down)
 Mood or behavioral regulation:
  • Difficulties going to sleep
  • Difficulties staying asleep throughout the night
  • Is a light sleeper
  • Frequent meltdowns
  • Moody
  • Irritable
  • Difficulty with adapting to changes
  • Gets frustrated easily
  • Has difficulty handling exciting events (parties, holidays, cannot be taken to a store or restaurant)
  • Difficulty calming down
  • Becomes too upset
  • Is easily overwhelmed
  • Is easily overstimulated
  • Problems calming self
  • Cannot play independently
  • Cannot entertain themself
  • Frequent behavioral outbursts
  • Mood lability
  • Is too intense at times
  • Is hyperresponsive to things
  • Gives up easily
  • Negative affect or irritability

Attention:

  • Difficulty shifting attention
  • Trouble staying focused
  • Difficulty maintaining focus on task at hand
  • Difficulty encoding new information
  • Difficulty focusing on new information
  • Difficulty sustaining mental effort

Impulse control:

  • Difficulty waiting
  • Does not think before responding
  • Difficulty complying with rules
  • Engages in risky behavior
  • Has poor impulse controls
  • Difficulty delaying gratification
  • Confabulating
  • Taking possessions of others
  • Frequently interrupting others
  • Needs constant supervision
  • Difficulty waiting turn
 Communication:
  • Delayed acquisition of language
  • Difficulty using language to express self so that the listener understands
  • Talking too much
  • Problems linking words with feelings
  • Making comments that seem out of context
  • Reduced verbal fluency
  • Difficulty following along in a conversation

Social:

  • Overly friendly with strangers
  • Gullible or naïve
  • Is unaware of personal space
  • Difficulty reading social cues
  • Difficulty understanding social consequences
  • Socially immature/acts too young
  • Difficulty attending to facial or social cues
  • Difficulty interacting with friends/peers
  • Is not aware of their actions on others

Daily living:

  • Struggles understanding time
  • Delayed toileting, feeding, or bathing
  • Persistent problems in carrying out common daily routines, such as getting dressed

Motor:

  • Problems in coordination and balance
  • Problems in integration of left and right motor activities
  • Delayed attainment of gross motor skills
  • Is accident prone
  • Poor fine motor skills (poor drawing and handwriting, difficulty manipulating buttons or zippers, difficulty using eating utensils)
  • Difficulty learning coordinated motor skills
  • Bumps into things frequently
  • Falls frequently
ND-PAE: neurobehavioral disorder associated with prenatal alcohol exposure.
