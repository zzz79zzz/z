Dominant sources of heat loss during surgery: Radiation and convection
The dominant sources of heat loss during surgery are radiation and convection, with evaporation and conduction from the skin surface contributing little. The extent to which evaporation from within surgical incisions contributes remains unknown.
Modified from: Sessler DI. Perioperative heat balance. Anesthesiology 2000; 92:578.
