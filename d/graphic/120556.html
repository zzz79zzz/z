Distinguishing what is considered a prebiotic with the proposed definition
The figure shows candidate as well as accepted prebiotics in that levels of evidence vary, with FOS and GOS being the most researched prebiotics. Prebiotics must be selectively utilized by host microorganisms and have adequate evidence of health benefit for the target host. Dietary prebiotics must not be degraded by the target host enzymes.
CLA: conjugated linoleic acid; PUFA: polyunsaturated fatty acid; FOS: fructooligosaccharides; GOS: galactooligosaccharides; MOS: mannanoligosaccharide; XOS: xylooligosaccharide.
