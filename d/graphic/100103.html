Contrast-enhanced MRI of the liver, demonstrating rim enhancement six weeks after transarterial chemoembolization (TACE), suggestive of disease recurrence
Axial image of a gadolinium-enhanced MRI of the liver performed 6 weeks after TACE. The lesion in segment 4 of the liver has central necrosis that is compatible with treatment; the thick rind of enhancement (arrows) is suggestive of residual disease. This lesion would be difficult to classify using any of the response evaluation criteria. The patient was retreated with a second TACE procedure.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
