Time course of acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
Schematic representation of the time course of the acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS). During the early (or exudative) phase, the lesion is characterized by high permeability pulmonary edema followed by the formation of hyaline membranes. After seven to ten days, a proliferative phase may develop, with marked interstitial inflammation, fibrosis, and disordered healing.
Redrawn from Katzenstein AA, Askin FB. Surgical Pathology of Non-neoplastic Lung Disease. Saunders, Philadelphia, 1982.
