Amlodipine, lisinopril, chlorthalidone, and rates of heart failure (HF) in hypertensive patients
In the ALLHAT trial, cumulative event rates for the development of heart failure (HF) according to primary treatment with chlorthalidone, amlodipine, or lisinopril. Compared to chlorthalidone, there were significant increases in the six year rate of HF with both amlodipine (10.2 versus 7.7 percent, relative risk 1.38, 95 percent CI 1.25 to 1.52) and lisinopril (8.7 versus 7.7 percent, relative risk 1.19 95 percent CI 1.07 to 1.31).
Data from The ALLHAT Officers, JAMA 2002; 288:2981.
