First cigarette raises blood pressure
Change in systolic blood pressure over 15 minutes after smoking the first cigarette of the day (blue squares) or during sham smoking (red circles) in 10 normotensive smokers. Smoking was associated with a 15 to 20 mmHg rise in systolic pressure that began to dissipate at 15 minutes.
Data from Gropelli, A, Giorgi, DM, Omboni, S, et al, J Hypertens 1992; 10:495.
