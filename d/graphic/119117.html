Magnetic resonance image of open-lip schizencephaly and polymicrogyria
Axial magnetization-prepared rapid acquisition gradient-echo (MPRAGE) magnetic resonance image demonstrates a large right-sided full-thickness cleft (asterisk), lined by abnormal polymicrogyric gray matter (arrows), that communicates with the lateral ventricle. Also, note absence of the septum pellucidum in this child with septo-optic dysplasia and polymicrogyria (dashed arrow) without schizencephaly in the left cerebral hemisphere.
From: Prabhu SP, Andronikou S, Vargas SO, Robertson RL. Brain. In: Pediatric Radiology: Practical Imaging Evaluation of Infants and Children, Lee EY (Ed), Wolters Kluwer, Philadelphia 2018.
