Chest radiograph in an infant with esophageal atresia and tracheoesophageal fistula
Esophageal atresia with a tracheoesophageal fistula at the distal one-third end of the trachea (type C). A nasogastric tube is coiled in the proximal esophagus, consistent with esophageal atresia. The gastrointestinal tract is distended, consistent with a communication between the trachea and distal esophagus.
