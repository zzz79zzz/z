Vertical band gastroplasty
Vertical banded gastroplasty is a purely restrictive procedure in which the upper part of the stomach is partitioned by a vertical staple line with a tight outlet wrapped by a prosthetic mesh or band. The small upper stomach pouch gets filled quickly by solid food and prevents consumption of a large meal. Weight loss occurs because of decreased caloric intake of solid food.
