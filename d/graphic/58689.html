Electrical activity in atrial fibrillation
During atrial fibrillation there are multiple reentrant circuits within the right and left atrium, resulting in nonuniform activitation of the atrial myocardium. These circuits, which produce multiple wavelets, often occur around the normal structures of the atrial including the orifices of the superior (SVC) and inferior (IVC) vena cavae, the orifice of the pulmonary veins (PVs), and the right (RAA) and left (LAA) atrial appendages.
