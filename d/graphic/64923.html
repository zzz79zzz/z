Amiodarone toxicity
Electron micrograph shows an alveolar macrophage with intracytoplasmic lamellar bodies typical of amiodarone toxicity. These phospholipid inclusions account for the foamy appearance of histiocytes and epithelial cells in patients taking amiodarone and can be identified even in patients who lack radiographic or clinical evidence of lung disease.
