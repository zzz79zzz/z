Intrauterine sucking lesions
Intrauterine sucking lesions can be seen in the dorsum of the fingers, hand, or radial aspect of the wrist of newborn infants. The lesions may appear as ruptured (pictured above) or intact vesicles. They can be multiple, unilateral, or bilateral. Rarely, they may have a hemorrhagic component.
