Complications of mucopolysaccharidoses
Complication Types of MPS
Respiratory
Central apnea from cord compression MPS IV A and B; also occurs in other types
Airway obstruction MPS I, II, VI, and VII
Sinopulmonary infectious All MPS types
Pulmonary function abnormalities MPS I, II, IVA, VI, and VII
Cardiac
Valvular disease MPS I, II, and VI; less commonly in MPS III A to D
Vascular disease MPS IH (untreated)
Pulmonary hypertension Secondary usually to severe OSA
Skeletal and connective tissue
Dysostosis multiplex MPS I, II, IV, VI, and VII; less extensive in MPS III
Odontoid hypoplasia MPS IV A and B; severe forms of MPS I and VII
Kyphoscoliosis MPS I, II (less common), IV, VI, and VII
Genu valgum Majority of patients with MPS IV A and B; can occur in other types
Short stature MPS I, II, IVA, VI, and VII
Joint stiffness and degenerative disease Nearly all MPS patients
Ligamentous laxity Most often in MPS IV A and B
Hernias Especially in MPS I, II, VI, and VII
Neurologic
Developmental delay and neurologic decline Severe forms of MPS I, II, III, and VII
Abnormal behavior Severe MPS II and III (less common in severe MPS I)
Seizures MPS II, MPS III; rarely seen in other MPS disorders
Sleep disturbance Common, especially in MPS II and III
Hydrocephalus MPS I, II, VI, and VII
Cervical cord compression MPS I, II (less common), IV, VI, and VII
Pachymeningitis cervicalis MPS I, II, VI, and VII
C1-C2 subluxation Common in MPS IV A and B; also occurs in MPS I, VI, and VII
Carpal tunnel syndrome MPS I, II, VI, and VII
Recurrent or chronic diarrhea MPS II and III
Ophthalmologic
Corneal clouding MPS I, IV, VI, and VII
Glaucoma MPS I, II, and VI
Optic neuropathy MPS I, II, and VI
Degeneration of the retina MPS I, II, III, VI, and VII
Otolaryngologic
Hearing loss All MPS types
MPS: mucopolysaccharidoses; OSA: obstructive sleep apnea.
