Gelatin Dissolve 1 teaspoon (5 grams) of gelatin powder (plain or flavored) in 5 mL of normal saline. Vaccine allergy practice parameter. Kelso JM, Li JT. Practice Parameters: Adverse reactions to vaccines. Ann Allergy Asthma Immunol 2009; 103:1.
Carmine Make 10% solution of carmine powder in phosphate-buffered saline. Agitate at 4°C for two hours. Dialyze against purified water, and filter through 0.22-micron filter. Anibarro B, Seoane J, Vila C, et al. Occupational asthma induced by inhaled carmine among butchers. Int J Occup Med Environ Health 2003; 16:133.
Annato Additive-containing food (Gouda cheese) and a commercial preparation of annato dye, diluted 1000-fold was used. Ebo DG, Ingelbrecht S, Bridts CH, et al. Allergy for cheese; evidence for an IgE-mediated reaction from the natural dye annatto. Allergy 2009; 64:1558.
Guar gum Additive-containing food (a meal substitute) and guar gum preparation (no details provided). Papanikolaou I, Stenger R, Bessot JC, et al. Anaphylactic shock to guar gum (food additive E412) contained in a meal substitute. Allergy 2007; 62:822.

Potassium metabisulfite Maximum concentration of 1 mg/mL. Bush RK, Zoratti E, Taylor SL. Diagnosis of sulfite and aspirin sensitivity. Clin Rev Allergy 1990; 8:159.

Carob bean gum Carob gum at 5 mg/mL and 25 mg/mL. Can use raw carob gum, boiled carob gum, and carob bean extract for skin testing also. Alarcon, E, del Pozo MD, Bartolome B, et al. J Investig Allergol Clin Immunol 2011; 21:77.
