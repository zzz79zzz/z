Survival after mitral valve surgery is better for patients in NYHA class I/II independent of procedure
Long-term patient survival after mitral valve surgery for chronic mitral regurgitation is better in those with NYHA class I/II compared to those in class III/IV at the time of surgery. This benefit is independent of the type of procedure performed (replacement versus repair), although the outcomes are better in those undergoing repair.
Data from Tribouilloy CM, Enriquez-Sarano M, Schaff HV, et al. Circulation 1999; 99:400.
