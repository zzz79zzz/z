Routine laboratory and bedside testsÂ for patients with life-threatening hemoptysis
Type and cross-matching of a blood specimen
Complete blood count (white blood cell count and differential, hemoglobin level, hematocrit, and platelet count)
Prothrombin time (or International Normalized Ratio [INR]) and partial thromboplastin time
Electrolytes, blood urea nitrogen, and creatinine
Arterial blood gas
Liver function tests
Urinalysis
Sputum Gram stain
Sputum culture (including Mycobacterium tuberculosis, fungi, and bacteria), and cytology
Echocardiography
Selected laboratory testing (when select conditions are suspected)
Toxicity screen (eg, cocaine, aspirin)
Platelet function testing
Brain natriuretic peptide (BNP) or N-terminal pro-BNP
Serologic tests - ANCA, ANA, double-stranded DNA antibodies, anti-GBM antibodies, RF, anticardiolipin antibodies
ANCA: anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies; ANA: anti-nuclear antibodies; anti-GBM antibodies: anti-glomerular basement membrane antibodies; RF: rheumatoid factor.
