Congenital cervical dysgenesis
The cervical body is intact (A) with obstruction of the cervical os. The cervical body (B) consists of a fibrous band of variable diameter that may contain endocervical glands. Fragmented portions of the cervix are noted (C) with no connection to the uterine body. Hypoplasia of the uterine cavity may be associated with cervical cords or fragmentation. The midportion of the cervix (D) is hypoplastic with a bulbous tip. No cervical lumen is identified.
