Initial management of lower urinary tract symptoms

DRE: digital rectal examination; LUTS: lower urinary tract symptoms; PSA: prostate-specific antigen.

* When life expectancy is >10 years and if the diagnosis of prostate cancer can modify the management.

¶ When significant nocturia is a predominant symptom.

Δ Assess and start treatment before referral.

◊ In practice, advise patients with symptoms to aim for a urine output of about 1 liter/24 hours.
