2015 Morphological Uterus Sonographic Assessment (MUSA) criteria for diagnosis of adenomyosis
Schematic drawings illustrating the ultrasound features considered currently to be typical of adenomyosis: asymmetrical thickening (A), cysts (B), hyperechoic islands (C), fan-shaped shadowing (D), echogenic subendometrial lines and buds (E), translesional vascularity (F), irregular junctional zone (G), and interrupted junctional zone (H). In an updated consensus article, published in 2022, a globular uterus was included as an indirect feature of adenomyosis.[1]
Reference:
