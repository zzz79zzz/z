NIDA principles of drug use disorder treatment in the criminal justice system
Drug addiction is a brain disease that affects behavior
Recovery from drug addiction requires effective treatment, followed by management of the problem over time
Treatment must last long enough to produce stable behavioral change
Assessment is the first step in treatment
Tailoring services to fit the needs of the individual is an important part of effective drug use disorder treatment for criminal justice populations
Drug use during treatment should be carefully monitored
Treatment should target factors that are associated with criminal behavior
Criminal justice supervision should incorporate treatment planning, and treatment providers should be aware of correctional supervision requirements
Continuity of care is essential during re-entry into the community
A balance of rewards and sanctions encourages prosocial behavior and treatment participation
Individuals with co-occurring drug use disorder and mental health problems often require an integrated treatment approach
Medications are an important part of treatment for many individuals with drug use disorder
Treatment planning for populations with criminal justice involvement and drug use disorder who are living in or re-entering the community should include strategies to prevent and treat serious, chronic medical conditions, such as HIV/AIDS, hepatitis B and C, and tuberculosis
NIDA: National Institute on Drug Abuse.
