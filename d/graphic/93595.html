CPB: cardiopulmonary bypass; CVP: central venous pressure; PAP: pulmonary artery pressure; PADP: pulmonary artery diastolic pressure; PAWP: pulmonary artery wedge pressure; LV: left ventricular; TEE: transesophageal echocardiography; RV: right ventricular.
* PAWP should not be measured prior to neutralizing heparin following CPB. Initially, PADP is measured, the PADP may overestimate PAWP when patients have elevated pulmonary vascular resistance (eg, pulmonary hypertension).
¶ PADP or PAWP are indirect measures of LV filling pressure. With RV dysfunction and dilation, ventricular septal shift may increase LV filling pressure despite low or normal LV filling volume.
