Gastroesophageal reflux in the postprandial period off and on acid suppressive therapy
The total number of reflux episodes remains unchanged while the number of acid reflux episodes decreases and the number of non-acid reflux episodes increases on therapy. This indicates that proton pump inhibitor (PPI) therapy only changes the composition of the postprandial refluxate without affecting the total number of reflux episodes.
Data from: Vela MF, Camacho-Lobato L, Srinivasan R, et al. Simultaneous intraesophagel impedance and pH measurement of acid and nonacid gastroesophageal reflux: effect of omeprazole. Gastroenterology 2001; 120:1599.
