Antibacterial agents for oral treatment of acute infectious cystitis in children older than two years and adolescents
Antimicrobial Regimen Maximum single dose
Amoxicillin*¶ 13.3 mg/kg 3 times per day 500 mg
Amoxicillin-clavulanate (immediate release formulations)* 22.5 mg amoxicillin/kg twice per day 875 mg amoxicillin
Cefdinir 14 mg/kg once per day 600 mg
Cefixime 4 mg/kg twice per day 200 mg
Cefpodoxime 5 mg/kg twice per day 200 mg
Cefprozil 15 mg/kg twice per day 500 mg
Ceftibuten 9 mg/kg once per day 400 mg
Cefuroxime 15 mg/kg twice per day 500 mg
Cephalexin 12.5 to 25 mg/kg twice per day 500 mg
NitrofurantoinΔ 1.25 to 1.75 mg/kg 4 times per day 100 mg
Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX)*Δ 4 to 6 mg trimethoprim/kg twice per day 160 mg TMP
Refer to UpToDate topic on treatment of cystitis in children for information regarding choice of antimicrobial therapy.

* Escherichia coli strains may be resistant to amoxicillin, amoxicillin-clavulanate, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole, and cephalexin; therapy should be determined based on local susceptibility patterns.

¶ May be indicated if Enterococcus is suspected based on Gram stain, culture, or clinical features (eg, underlying genitourinary abnormality, recurrent urinary tract infection).

Δ May be indicated if Staphylococcus saprophyticus is suspected based on Gram stain, culture, or clinical features (sexually active female adolescent).
