Intestinal tuberculosis - CT enteroclysis I
A 40-year-old male patient presented with recurrent episodes of abdominal pain for one year with an episode of partial bowel obstruction, which was treated conservatively. Coronal reformatted images from CT enteroclysis demonstrate contiguous ileocecal involvement with ileal stricture (arrows) and large pericecal and mesenteric lymph nodes (asterisk) and small bowel feces sign (delta). These features are suggestive of intestinal tuberculosis.
CT: computed tomography.
