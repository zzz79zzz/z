Steps for successful integration of comprehensive adolescent preventive services into routine medical care
1. Conduct a needs assessment
How many adolescent patients are seen in the practice?
- What are their ages?
- What are their problems?
What are the current preventive service practice patterns of the practitioners?
2. Determine clinic readiness to make a systems change
Are adolescent preventive services an important aspect of care for the staff and the clinic?
Are adequate resources available, such as health education materials and questionnaires?
Are the administrators supportive of change?
3. Identify a staff prevention coordinator and specify roles and responsibilities:
Setting goals for the implementation process
Coordinating implementation activities
Establishing a tracking system
Ensuring new staff are trained
Conducting quality assurance
4. Identify a set of preventive services guidelines to be used by the clinic
5. Develop clinic procedures and protocols to address the following issues:
How will privacy and confidentiality be handled?
How will screening questionnaires be used?
Who will provide health guidance? Health risk reduction counseling?
6. Provide staff training and preparation
7. Identify and promote mechanisms to maximize reimbursement for preventive services
Appropriate use of Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) codes
8. Establish school and community referral sources
