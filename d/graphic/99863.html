Misplaced Foley catheter on CT
A CT scan reformatted in the sagittal plane (A) shows a Foley catheter (arrow) with balloon (delta) impacted into the base of the penile urethra. The prostate gland (asterisk) is cranial to the Foley catheter. The urinary bladder (bl) is significantly distended. Image B is an axial image through the base of the penis, and the distended balloon of the Foley catheter (delta) is located at the base of the penis. Image C is a coronal view of the pelvis and shows the Foley balloon (delta) below the prostate (asterisk).
CT: computed tomography.
