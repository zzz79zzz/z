Lung ultrasound showing B lines
This movie depicts normal lung sliding with the additional finding of B-lines. B-lines are an abnormal finding and indicate increased lung density due to pulmonary edema, pus, intraparenchymal hemorrhage, or other pathology. B-lines are perpendicular to the pleura, extend to the bottom of the image without fading, and move synchronously with respiratory movements.
