Kidney allograft biopsy showing hyperacute rejection with IgM by immunofluorescence microscopy
Immunofluorescence microscopy in hyperacute rejection showing the almost pathognomonic deposition of IgM around the wall of a small artery. The lumen (L) is markedly narrowed by intraluminal fibrin deposition (arrow), which comprises most of the space between the remaining lumen and the immunoglobulin positive vascular wall.
IgM: immunoglobulin M.
