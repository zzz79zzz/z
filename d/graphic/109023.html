Cystic hygroma with hydrops
The large fluid collection at the level of the neck is a cystic hygroma. Generalized subcutaneous fluid (lymphangiectasia, a very poor prognostic sign) is indicated by the arrows. Bilateral pleural effusions are also present.
CRL: crown-rump length; GA: gestational age.
