Femoral artery emboli on CT scan
A transverse CT scan through the pelvis (A) shows nonocclusive thrombus in the left superficial femoral and deep femoral arteries (circle). (B) is a magnified view of (A) and shows nonocclusive thrombus in the SFA (arrowhead) and in the DFA (arrow). (C) is a magnified sagittal reformat of the CT scan and shows the thrombus straddling the bifurcation (dashed arrow) and extending into the SFA (arrowhead) and DFA (arrow).
CT: computed tomography; SFA: superficial femoral artery; DFA: deep femoral artery.
