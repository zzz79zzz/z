Balloon extraction of common bile duct stones
Left panel shows a balloon catheter being threaded over a guidewire within the common bile duct (arrows). At least two gallstones are visible in the distal common bile duct (arrowheads), which is dilated. In the right panel, the balloon has been inflated above a gallstone (arrow), which permits the balloon to drag the gallstone out of the duct.
