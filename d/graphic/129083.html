Posterior cervical lymphadenopathy in human African trypanosomiasis (HAT)
In gambiense HAT, lymphadenopathy typically occurs in posterior cervical nodes. Painless enlargement of the soft, mobile posterior cervical nodes is classically referred to as "Winterbottom's sign." The lymph node aspirate is used for diagnosis.
