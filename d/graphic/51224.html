Direct inguinal hernia
Direct inguinal hernias are medial and inferior to the deep epigastric vessels. As seen in this transverse section of the anterior abdominal wall, the hernia passes directly through the external inguinal ring and does not traverse the inguinal canal.
Adapted from O'Rahilly. Abdominal walls. In: Basic Human Anatomy, W.B. Saunders Company, Philadelphia 1983. p.230.
