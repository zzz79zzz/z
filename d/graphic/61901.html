Rheumatic mitral regurgitation
The short axis view during diastole (D) shows a mildly stenotic mitral orifice (MVO) by area (4 cm2), which presented no significant obstruction to mitral inflow. The valve is shown during systole in the lower panel (arrows). The medial side of the valve has failed to coapt; in rheumatic mitral regurgitation, failure of coaptation of this degree is usually associated with significant mitral regurgitation.
LV: left ventricle; RV: right ventricle.
