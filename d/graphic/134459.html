Child and adult current asthma prevalence by age and sex: United States, 2017
Among children, current asthma prevalence was higher among male children aged 0 to 4 years (6.8%) compared with female children in the same age group (2%). Asthma prevalence was similar for male and female children aged 5 to 24 years. Among adults 25 years and older, the percent of current asthma was higher among women than men.
Reproduced from: National Center for Environmental Health, Division of Environmental Health Science and Practice.
