Choice of antihypertensive monotherapy does not predict outcome
In the ALLHAT trial, cumulative event rates for the primary outcome (fatal coronary heart disease or nonfatal myocardial infarction) according to primary treatment with chlorthalidone, amlodipine, or lisinopril. Compared to chlorthalidone, there was no significant difference with amlodipine (relative risk 0.98) or lisinopril (relative risk 0.99 percent) at a mean of 4.9 years.
Data from: The ALLHAT Officers, JAMA 2002; 288:2981.
