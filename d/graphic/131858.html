Typical T2 lesions on brain MRI in patients with myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein antibody-associated disease (MOGAD)
(A) Axial FLAIR brain MRI reveals T2 hyperintensity in the right cerebellar peduncle (arrow). (B) Axial T2-weighted brain MRI reveals T2 hyperintensities in the thalamus bilaterally (arrows). (C) Axial FLAIR brain MRI reveals T2 hyperintensities in the right midbrain and internal capsule (arrows). (D) Axial FLAIR MRI reveals multifocal T2 hyperintensities in the white matter (arrows) characteristic of the acute disseminated encephalomyelitis (ADEM) phenotype of MOGAD.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging; FLAIR: fluid-attenuated inversion recovery.
