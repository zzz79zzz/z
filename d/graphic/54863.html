Differences between cyclic and chronic temporal patterns of vomiting
Differences between cyclic and chronic temporal patterns of vomiting can be compared when the number of emeses is plotted over a 2-month period. The chronic pattern (red line) is characterized by low-grade, nearly daily episodes, whereas the cyclic pattern (black line) is marked by high-intensity episodes every several weeks.
Adapted from: Li BU. New hope for children with cyclic vomiting syndrome. Contemp Pediatr 2002; 19:121.
