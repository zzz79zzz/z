Ventilatory response to hypoxemia
Ventilation increases in a curvilinear fashion as PO2 falls. The data are obtained with PaCO2 held constant. Under usual circumstances, the increased ventilation associated with hypoxemia results in a drop in PaCO2, which blunts the ventilatory response. Note that ventilation does not significantly increase until PO2 is less than 60 mmHg.
Adapted from Hudgel DW, Control of breathing. In: Bronchial Asthma: Mechanisms and Therapeutics, 2d ed, Weiss EB, Segal MS, Stein M (Eds), Little, Brown, Boston, 1984, with permission.
