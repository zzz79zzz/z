Gram-negative bacteria

Bacteroides fragilis group: B. fragilis, B. thetaiotaomicron, B. distasonis, B. ovatus, B. vulgatus*

Bacteroides non-fragilis group: B. uniformis, B. cacae, B. eggerthii, B. finegoldii, B. stercoris, B. dorei
Parabacteroides spp: P. distasonis, P. goldsteinii, P. gordonii, P. johnsonii, P. merdae
Pigmented Prevotella (formerly Bacteroides): P. intermedia, P. melaninogenica, P. corporis, P. denticola, P. loescheii, P. nigrescens
Prevotella (formerly Bacteroides): P. bivia (formerly B. bivius), P. disiens (formerly B. disiens), P. oralis (formerly B. oralis)
Porphyromonas spp (eg, P. asaccharolyticus, formerly B. asaccharolyticus)
Alistipes spp
Fusobacterium: F. nucleatum, F. necrophorum, F. varium
Gram-positive cocci
Peptostreptococcus: P. intermedius, P. micros, P. anaerobius, P. magnus, P. asaccharolyticus, P. prevotii¶
Gram-positive, spore-forming bacilli
Clostridium: C. perfringens, C. sporogenes, C. sordellii, C. septicum, C. tertium, C. ramosum, C. novyi, C. histolyticum, C. bifermentans, C. innocuum, C. tetani, C. botulinum
Clostridioides: C. (formerly Clostridium) difficile
Gram-positive, non-spore-forming bacilliΔ
Actinomyces: A. israeli, A. naeslundii, A. odontolyticus, A. viscosus
Cutibacterium (formerly Propionibacterium): C. acnes
Eggerthella spp (E. lenta)
Faecalibacterium spp
Lactobacillus spp
Gram-negative cocci
Veillonella: Veillonella parvula

* The B. fragilis group is comprised of >50 species.

¶ The Streptococcus anginosus group is not anaerobic gram-positive streptococci but can grow under anaerobic conditions.

Δ Clinically relevant gram-positive, non-spore-forming bacilli include genera from both the Actinobacteria and Firmicutes phyla.
