Factors relevant to pseudocyesis that can be a focus of counseling
Contributory factors Focus of counseling Examples
Strong desire for a baby Identify what fuels the wish; redirect energy toward other ways to achieve similar goals Helping a socially isolated woman focus on making new friends[1]
Misconceptions about fertility Psychoeducation Teaching a woman that it is the man's sperm that determines a baby's sex, so she is not "to blame" for having only girl babies[2]
Lack of awareness of emotions; use of denial as a primary coping mechanism Help patient recognize and articulate emotions Helping a woman voice anger at her husband for pressuring her to have a tubal ligation[3]
Helplessness (eg, due to poverty and/or abuse) Focus on aspects of her life that patient can control Helping a woman find resources to leave an abusive relationship[4]
Low self esteem Help patient identify her strengths Helping a woman notice how she has social value without being pregnant[5]
Losses Grief counseling Working through suppressed feelings about a past miscarriage[1]
Cognitive bias – "jumping to conclusions" and not questioning conclusions Cognitive reframing Helping a woman consider other explanations for physical sensations[2]
