Cavernous transformation of portal vein thrombosis
Contrast-enhanced CT scan of the upper abdomen in a 45-year-old male alcoholic demonstrates multiple periportal collateral vessels (small arrows) which have developed secondary to chronic portal vein thrombosis. The portal vein thrombosed due to chronic pancreatitis; a small pancreatic pseudocyst is seen in the body of the pancreas (large arrow).
CT: computed tomography.
