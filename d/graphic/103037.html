Mechanism of Lisfranc (tarsometatarsal) injury
Indirect Lisfranc injuries often result from an axial load placed on a plantar-flexed foot that then forcibly rotates, bends, or is compressed. This mechanism can occur in field sports when a player inadvertently steps or falls onto the heel of an opponent whose foot is forcibly plantar-flexed
