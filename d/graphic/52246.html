Time course of lacunar infarction
Penetrating artery occlusions usually cause symptoms that develop over a short period of time, hours or at most a few days, compared to large artery-related brain ischemia which can evolve over a longer period. A stuttering course may ensue, as with large artery thrombosis. This patient had a pure motor hemiparesis.
