Algorithm for evaluating an infant with frequent regurgitation
Dotted line denotes alternate pathway, depending on results of clinical assessment. Bold font denotes diagnosis.
GERD: gastroesophageal reflux disease; CBC: complete blood count; BUN: blood urea nitrogen; tTG-IgA: immunoglobulin A antibodies to tissue transglutaminase; PPI: proton pump inhibitor; IgA: immunoglobulin A.
* Warning signals include bilious vomiting, consistently forceful vomiting, abdominal tenderness or distension, fever, or other systemic signs (refer to topic text).
¶ Trials of both thickened feeds and a cow's milk- and soy-free diet should generally be performed before a trial of acid suppression. If neither of these trials is successful, the next step is a trial of acid suppression.
Δ Celiac screen is only useful if the diet contains wheat, barley, or rye. The screen usually consists of tTg-IgA (often with total IgA to exclude IgA deficiency).
