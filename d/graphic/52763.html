Atrial flutter
Atrial flutter (at an atrial rate of approximately 240 cycles/min) with a variable degree of atrioventricular block. The typical flutter (F) waves have a uniform, sawtooth appearance and occur at regular intervals that distinguish them from irregular fibrillatory waves in atrial fibrillation. (The negative polarity of the flutter waves in lead II is consistent with the typical "counterclockwise" reentrant mechanism.)
