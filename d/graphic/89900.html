Liver infarction subcapsular hematoma HELLP syndrome on CT scan
(A) An axial CT scan through the upper abdomen shows a large subcapsular hematoma compressing the liver (arrow).
(B) This image shows a large and irregular perfusion defect involving the right lobe and part of the left lobe of the liver (arrows).
CT: computed tomography; HELLP: hemolysis, elevated liver enzymes, and low platelets.
