Right anterior oblique projection of left ventricular angiogram in tetralogy of Fallot
An LV angiogram with the camera in a right anterior oblique projection in tetralogy of Fallot does not permit good visualization of the VSD; however, the left-to-right flow across the VSD does allow excellent visualization of the hypertrophied INF and the severe subpulmonary obstruction (*).
MPA: main pulmonary artery; INF: infundibulum; LV: left ventricle; VSD: ventricular septal defect.
