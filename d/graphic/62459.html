Reversion of AF with antiarrhythmic drugs is related to arrhythmia duration
In a study comparing two doses of intravenous ibutilide with intravenous sotalol for acute reversion of atrial fibrillation, the rate of successful reversion was inversely related to the duration of the arrhythmia prior to therapy.
Data from: Vos MA, Golitsyn SR, Stangl K, et al for the Ibutilide/Sotalol Comparator Study Group, Heart 1998; 79:568.
