Flow-volume loop and degree of upper airway narrowing
Volume (as liters [L] from total lung capacity [TLC]) is plotted against inspiratory and expiratory flows. The blue line (C) is the control effort; the number on each curve refers to the orifice diameter in mm. Lesions must narrow the tracheal lumen to less than 8 mm before abnormalities can be detected by spirometry.
TLC: total lung capacity; RV: residual volume.
Redrawn from Miller RD, Hyatt RE. Obstructing lesions of the larynx and trachea: clinical and physiologic characteristics. Mayo Clin Proc 1969; 44:145.
