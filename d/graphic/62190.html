Sliding versus paraesophageal hiatus hernia
Distinction between a sliding hiatus hernia (type I) and paraesophageal hernia (type II). With type I hernia, the leading edge is the gastric cardia while with type II it is the gastric fundus. The squamocolumnar junction maintains its native position in the paraesophageal hernia while it is displaced upward with the sliding hernia.
SC: squamocolumnar.
