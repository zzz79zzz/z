Pleural fluid analysis for cholesterol effusions
Appearance
  • Commonly milky, opaque, or turbid; less commonly serous, oily yellow, bloody
Protein
  • >3 g/dL (>30 g/L), consistent with an exudate
Glucose
  • Normal (60 to 100 mg/dL [3.3 and 5.6 mmol/L])
  • Low (<60 mg/dL [<3.3 mmol/L]) in rheumatoid arthritis, malignancy, tuberculous pleurisy, paragonimiasis
LDH
  • >500 IU/L, consistent with an exudate
  • >1000 IU/L in rheumatoid arthritis, malignancy, tuberculous pleurisy, paragonimiasis
pH
  • <7.4; may be very low (eg, <7.2) in rheumatoid arthritis, malignancy, tuberculous pleurisy, paragonomiasis
White cell count
  • Low (<5000 cells/microL); lymphocyte predominance; eosinophilia if parasites are responsible for the effusion
Cholesterol
  • >200 mg/dL (5.18 mmol/L)
Triglycerides
  • <110 mg/dL (1.24 mmol/L)
Cholesterol:triglyceride ratio
  • >1
Chylomicrons
  • Absent
Cholesterol crystals
  • Present (under polarizing light)
LDH: lactate dehydrogenase; IU: international units.
