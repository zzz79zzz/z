Ion redistribution after acid load
Effect of an HCl load on the distribution of Cl–, Na+, and K+. As H+ enters the cells to be buffered, intracellular Na+ and K+ leave the cells and move into the extracellular fluid, tending to raise the plasma K+ concentration. These ion shifts are reversed when H+ are removed from the extracellular fluid.
HCl: hydrogen chloride; H+: hydrogen; Cl–: chloride; Na+: sodium; K+: potassium.
