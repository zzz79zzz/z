Surgical treatment of acute eponychia

Surgical drainage is indicated for patients with acute eponychia who present late and have an abscess cavity. The drawings illustrate the appropriate drainage procedure.

(Left) Preoperative appearance and incision.

(Center) Eponychium elevated as a flap to drain the underlying abscess.

(Right) A small piece of xeroform is inserted into the wound to keep the eponychial flap open and allow the wound to drain.
