Resources for military families affected by parental/caregiver deployment

American Academy of Pediatrics

www.aap.org/en/patient-care/military-families-and-deployment/

Provides resources for military families and professionals caring for military families; the Uniformed Services Section has published a clinical report on the health and mental health needs of United Stated military families:

http://pediatrics.aappublications.org/content/143/1/e20183258

Military Child Education Coalition

www.militarychild.org

Nonprofit organization that increases awareness of and implements programs to meet the challenges facing military children

Military Kids Connect[1]

Provides resources to prepare children for an upcoming move

Military One Source

www.militaryonesource.mil

(telephone: 1-800-342-9647)

Provides a range of services to active duty and reserve members and their families (including help with child care, mental health, personal finances, resilience, relocation information, etc)

National Guard Family Services

https://www.nationalguard.mil/About-the-Guard/Army-National-Guard/Resources/Family-Services

Provides links to resources for National Guard families living away from military installations

National Military Family Association Operation Purple Program

www.militaryfamily.org/kids-operation-purple/camps

Provides free summer camps for children and adolescents experiencing parental deployment

Sesame Street Workshop: Talk, listen, connect

www.sesamestreetformilitaryfamilies.org/

A multimedia outreach program to support military families with young children experiencing deployments, combat-related injury, or death

Tragedy Assistance Program for Survivors

www.taps.org

Provides assistance for families who are grieving the death of a military service member

Zero to Three Coming Together Around Military Families

https://www.zerotothree.org/our-work/military-families/

Aims at strengthening the resilience of young children and families who are experiencing deployment
