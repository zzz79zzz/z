Entrainment in typical atrial flutter with right atrial pacing with the rate of recording by the ECG machine being slowed by one-half
The spontaneous rhythm, in which the flutter waves are not clearly apparent, is shown in the left side of panel A. The right atrium is then paced at just under 400 beats/min; entrainment is achieved at the asterisk when there is a sudden change in atrial morphology as the flutter rate matched the pacing rate. Cessation of pacing led to conversion to sinus rhythm (panel B).
