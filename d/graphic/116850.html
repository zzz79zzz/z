Concentric and bottleneck airway obstruction
Concentric airway obstruction (A) is a narrow ring-like area of obstruction that involves a short segment of the airway as opposed to bottleneck obstruction (B and C) which involves a longer segment with tapering superiorly and inferiorly. Bottleneck lesions are less likely to be responsive to balloon dilation since they are more likely to be associated with loss of supporting cartilage.
Modified from: Sheski FD, Mathur PN. Balloon Dilation of Benign Tracheobronchial Stenosis. In: Interventional Pulmonology, 1st ed, Beamis JK Jr, Mathur PN (Eds), McGraw-Hill 1999.
