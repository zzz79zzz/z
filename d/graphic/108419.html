Summary of health risks and consequences of human trafficking
Physical health
  • Intentional traumatic injuries (eg, fractures, cuts, and disfigurement) and chemical/thermal burns
  • Accidental traumatic and overuse injuries (eg, joint sprains, disc herniations, muscles tears, and eye strain)
  • Chemical (eg, pesticides and industrial glues) and environmental exposures (eg, heat exhaustion and vision/hearing impairment)
  • Communicable diseases (eg, hepatitis A virus, intestinal parasites, typhoid, and tuberculosis)
  • Untreated chronic diseases (eg, anemia, cancer, chronic kidney disease, cardiovascular disease, diabetes mellitus, and hypertension)
  • Poor oral health and injuries (eg, tooth decay/fractures and mandibular dislocations)
  • Dehydration, malnutrition, and eating disorders
  • Branding/tattooing and skin disorders (eg, lice, scabies, and mycoses)
  • Complications from substance use disorders
  • Homicide and accidental death (eg, fatal trauma)
Reproductive health
  • Vaginal, perineal, and rectal injuries (eg, secondary/gang rape and use of foreign objects)
  • Sexually transmitted infections (eg, HIV, human papillomavirus, Neisseria gonorrhea, chlamydia, and hepatitis B and C viruses)
  • Diseases due to untreated sexually transmitted infections (eg, AIDS, cervical cancer, pelvic inflammatory disease, infertility, liver disease, and hepatocellular cancer)
  • Poor access to barrier protection or other contraception
  • Forced sterilization or use of contraceptive devices
  • Unplanned and high-risk pregnancies
  • Unsafe, forced abortions
  • Abortion-related complications
  • Forced pregnancy and childbirth
  • Lack of prenatal care, including prevention of vertical HIV transmission
Mental health
  • Stockholm syndrome (ie, traumatic bonding with perpetrator)
  • Affective disorders (eg, anxiety, depression, and panic attacks)
  • Posttraumatic stress disorder (symptoms include flashbacks, intrusive thoughts, memory difficulties, and hypervigilance) and complex posttraumatic stress disorder
  • Dissociative disorders involving varying degrees of detachment from self/reality
  • Sleep disorders (eg, sleep disturbances, nightmares, and insomnia)
  • Psychosomatic syndromes (eg, chronic pain, fatigue, gastrointestinal distress, headaches, and dizziness)
  • Low self-esteem, shame, guilt, self-loathing, and hopelessness
  • Forced or coerced substance use disorders
  • High-risk and self-injurious behaviors
  • Suicide and accidental death (eg, drug overdose)
HIV: human immunodeficiency virus; AIDS: acquired immunodeficiency syndrome.
