Compression bandage placement
Shown is a basic example of the application of a compression bandage for the treatment of chronic venous insufficiency. The bandage is applied from the toes to the tibial tuberosity, moving from distal to proximal in an overlapping manner. Care is taken to avoid bunching or wrinkling of the dressing during application which can cause localized pressure. Multilayer compression bandages have two to four layers including a layer of soft padding and at least one elastic layer; each layer is applied in a similar manner.
