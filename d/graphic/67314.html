Pincer mechanism
The pincer mechanism is one mechanism of injury that is proposed to explain the pathogenesis of cervical cord neurapraxia. It is hypothesized that during extension the cervical cord becomes compressed between the posterior margin of one vertebral body and the anterior margin of the spinous process of the vertebra below it. Such compression is most likely to occur in a hyperextension injury with axial loading.
