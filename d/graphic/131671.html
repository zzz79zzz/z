Different positions for breastfeeding

All breastfeeding positions have the following in common:

(A, B, and C) Infant and mother are comfortable, and the infant is positioned so that:

  • Abdomen is tucked flat against the mother
  • Body and head are in alignment (head not turned)
  • Head is tilted up and away from the chest, such that the infant is "reaching up" to the breast

(D) Infant latches on with chin buried in the breast, and the nose is free.
