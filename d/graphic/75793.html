Sezary cells in mycosis fungoides
Left panel: Peripheral blood smear from a 58-year-old woman with a two-year history of generalized erythroderma, illustrating the "cerebriform" shape of the nucleus of this circulating Sezary cell. Right panel: Electron micrograph showing the markedly irregular nucleus with a single prominent nucleolus.
From Brunning, RD, McKenna, RW. Tumors of the bone marrow. Atlas of tumor pathology (electronic fascicle), Third series, fascicle 9, 1994, Washington, DC. Armed Forces Institute of Pathology.
