Infectious agents that can cause a clinical picture of encephalitis in children

Viruses
(pathogens that can be treated with specific antiviral therapy are indicated with a *)

 Bacteria
(all of these pathogens can be treated with specific antibiotic therapy)
Most common causes
  • Enteroviruses (echovirus, coxsackievirus A and B, poliovirus, and the numbered enteroviruses)
  • Parechoviruses
  • Herpes simplex virus (type 1 or 2)*
  • Human herpesvirus 6*
  • Influenza virus*
  • Epstein-Barr virus
  • Cytomegalovirus*
  • Arboviruses (LaCrosse virus, West Nile virus, St. Louis encephalitis virus*, Eastern and Western equine encephalitis virus, Japanese encephalitis virus)
  • Varicella zoster virus*

Other less common causes
  • Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
  • Hendra virus
  • Hepatitis E virus
  • Herpes B virus
  • HIV*
  • Measles virus
  • Mumps virus
  • Murray Valley encephalitis virus
  • Nipah virus
  • Parvovirus B19
  • Powassan virus
  • Rabies virus
  • Rubella virus
  • Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2)*
  • Tick-borne encephalitis virus
  • Mycoplasma pneumoniae
  • Bartonella henselae (cat scratch disease)
  • Rickettsia rickettsii (Rocky Mountain spotted fever)
  • Borrelia burgdorferi (Lyme disease)
  • Coxiella burnetii (Q fever)
  • Listeria monocytogenes
  • Ehrlichia chaffeensis (human monocytotrophic ehrlichiosis)
  • Anaplasma phagocytophilum (human granulocytotrophic ehrlichiosis)
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Treponema pallidum
  • Bartonella quintana ("trench fever")
  • Tropheryma whipplei
Fungi
(all of these pathogens can be treated with specific antifungal therapy)
  • Cryptococcus neoformans
  • Coccidioides species
  • Histoplasma capsulatum
Parasites
(all of these pathogens can be treated with specific therapy)
  • Toxoplasma gondii
  • Plasmodium falciparum
  • Naegleria fowleri
  • Acanthamoeba spp
  • Balamuthia mandrillaris
  • Taenia solium (cysticercosis)
  • Baylisascaris procyonis
  • Gnathostoma spinigerum
  • Trypanosoma brucei gambiense
* Viral pathogens that can be treated with specific antiviral therapy.
