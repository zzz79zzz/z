Vascular damage control with temporary shunts in the proximal radial and ulnar artery
The picture shows vascular shunts placed into the proximal right radial and ulnar arteries just distal to the bifurcation of the brachial artery. The shunts allow ongoing perfusion to the distal upper extremity until definitive repair of the vascular injury can be performed.
