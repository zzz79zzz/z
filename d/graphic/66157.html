Radionuclide imaging of Chagas cardiomyopathy
Images of a 63-year-old man presenting with syncope due to episodes of sustained ventricular tachycardia with Chagas cardiomyopathy and normal echocardiogram. This figure shows 99mTc sestamibi myocardial perfusion SPECT images for assessment of myocardial viability and 123I MIBG myocardial SPECT (lower rows) for assessment of cardiac sympathetic innervation. There is a large defect in 123I MIBG uptake involving the inferior and apical walls with corresponding normal 99mTc sestamibi uptake, consistent with viable but denervated myocardium.
SPECT: single-photon emission computed tomography.
