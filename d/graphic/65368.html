Window period of acute HBV infection
Schematic representation of the serologic findings during the window period of acute hepatitis B virus infection. The disappearance of HBsAg (hepatitis B surface antigen) is followed by the appearance of anti-HBs. In some patients, however, anti-HBs may not be detectable until after a window period of several weeks to months. At this time, neither HBsAg nor anti-HBs can be detected, the serologic diagnosis may be made by the detection of IgM antibodies against hepatitis B core antigen (IgM anti-HBc).
* Window period.
