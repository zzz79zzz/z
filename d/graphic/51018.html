Surgery to treat bladder cancer in men (cystectomy)
Some people with bladder cancer must have surgery to remove the bladder and nearby organs. In men, the surgeon removes the bladder, the prostate gland, and the seminal vesicles (shown inside the dotted line). The prostate gland and the seminal vesicles each make some of the fluid that makes up semen.
