Power Doppler duplex imaging
Power Doppler duplex imaging identifies a blood vessel (arrows) adjacent to the brachial plexus in the groove between the anterior and middle scalene muscles in the posterior cervical triangle. In the interscalene region, the contributions to the brachial plexus are often monofascicular or oligofascicular (dashed arrows) and can be difficult to distinguish from adjacent muscle or blood vessels.
