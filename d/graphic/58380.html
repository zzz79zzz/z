Janeway lesion in infective endocarditis
A Janeway lesion (arrow) occurred on the palm in this patient with bacterial endocarditis due to Streptococcus bovis. These lesions are macular, nonpainful, and erythematous; they are located on the palms and soles.
Courtesy of Jan V Hirschmann. The Skin and Infection: A Color Atlas and Text, Sanders CV, Nesbitt LT Jr (Eds), Williams & Wilkins, Baltimore 1995.
