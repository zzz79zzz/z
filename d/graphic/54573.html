Two-dimensional transthoracic echocardiogram (2D TTE) from the parasternal short axis view at the level of the coronary arteries
The origins of the right (RCA) and left (LCA) coronary arteries can be seen on the short axis precordial view, obtained through the aortic root (Ao) just above the valves; there is a vague "pinwheel" relationship of the sweep of the arteries. Additionally, the left atrial appendage (LAA) is seen just inferior to the LCA and the pulmonary valve (PV) just superior.
LA: left atrium.
