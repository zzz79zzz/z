Obstructive apnea
The absence of signal in the nasal pressure transducer and near absence of signal in the thermistor identifies the event as an apnea. The continuing effort in the thoracic and abdominal leads indicates that it is an obstructive apnea. Note that ongoing effort in either of these leads would be sufficient to label this as an obstructive event.
