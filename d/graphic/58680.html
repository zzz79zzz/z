Severe myocardial ischemia
Sinus rhythm is present with a prolonged PR interval (first degree AV block). Severe myocardial ischemia is indicated by prominent ST depressions, except, notably, lead aVR which shows ST segment elevations. This combination of findings may be due to extensive ischemia (subendocardial and possibly basal transmural), raising concern for high-grade three-vessel and/or left main coronary artery disease.
