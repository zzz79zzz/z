Pediatric donor management goals
Hemodynamic support Blood pressure (mmHg)
  • Normalization of blood pressure
    • Systolic blood pressure appropriate for age
    • NOTE: Lower systolic blood pressures may be acceptable if biomarkers such as lactate are normal
  • CVP <12 (if measured)
  • Minimize vasoactive and inotropic support
  • Normal serum lactate
   Systolic* Diastolic
Neonate 60 to 90 35 to 60
Infants
(6 months)		 80 to 95 50 to 65
Toddler
(2 years)		 85 to 100 50 to 65
School age
(7 years)		 90 to 115 60 to 70
Adolescent
(15 years)		 110 to 130 65 to 80
Oxygenation and ventilation Fluids and electrolytes
  • Maintain PaO2 >100 mmHg
  • FiO2 0.40
  • Normalize PaCO2 35 to 45 mmHg
  • Arterial pH 7.30 to 7.45
  • Tidal volumes 8 to 10 cc/kg
  • PEEP 5 cm H2O
  • Serum Na+ 130 to 150 mEq/L
  • Serum K+ 3 to 5 mEq/L
  • Serum glucose 60 to 150 mg/dL
  • Ionized Ca++ (if measured) 0.8 to 1.2 mmol/L
Thermal regulation
  • Core body temperature 36 to 38°C

CVP: central venous pressure; FiO2: fraction of inspired oxygen; PaCO2: arterial carbon dioxide tension; PaO2: arterial oxygen tension; PEEP: positive end-expiratory pressure.

* Normal systolic blood pressure = 80 + (Age in years × 2).
