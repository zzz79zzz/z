Nodular regenerative hyperplasia
A more common form of idiopathic noncirrhotic portal hypertension than obliterative portal venopathy, nodular regenerative hyperplasia, as shown in this needle biopsy, is characterized by nodules composed of hyperplastic hepatocytes that are bordered by atrophic hepatocyte plates. The portal tract is located in the middle, termed reverse lobulation.
