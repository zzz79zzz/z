Despite warnings of potential for cross-reaction contained in the manufacturers' labeling of the nonantimicrobial sulfonamides listed in this table, there is minimal evidence for cross-reactivity for these agents in patients who are allergic to sulfonamide antimicrobials.
​Sulfasalazine is an exception and can cause a cross-reaction in patients with allergies to sulfonamide antimicrobials because it releases sulfapyridine (a cross-reactive arylamine sulfonamide) in contact with gut flora. Thus, use of sulfasalazine is contraindicated in patients with a significant allergy to sulfonamide antimicrobials.
HIV: human immunodeficiency virus; HCV: chronic hepatitis C virus infection; G6PD: glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase.
* US Food and Drug Administration (FDA)-approved product information for this drug contains cautionary statement(s) regarding use in patients with history of allergy to sulfonamide drugs. However, clear evidence of cross-reactivity to nonantimicrobial drugs listed in this table among patients with a history of hypersensitivity to sulfonamide antibiotics is lacking, except sulfasalazine.
¶ High risk in G6PD deficiency. See: http://www.g6pd.org/.
