Arteries of the neck
The vertebral arteries arise from the subclavian arteries. They angle backwards to the transverse processes of C6 and run superiorly through the transverse foramina of C6-C1 to the inferior surface of the skull. The vertebral arteries join together at the base of the brain to form the basilar artery.
