Differential diagnosis of myelin oligodendrocyte glycoprotein antibody-associated disease (MOGAD)
Genetic
  • Hereditary leukodystrophies
  • Hereditary spastic paraparesis
  • Leber hereditary optic neuropathy
  • Mitochondrial disorders (eg, MELAS)
Infectious (extensive list but most common listed here)
Bacterial/mycobacterial
  • Bartonella neuroretinitis, lyme, syphilis, tuberculosis
Fungal
  • Blastomycoses, coccidioides, cryptococcus, histoplasma, aspergillus
Viral
  • Acute flaccid myelitis with enterovirus, CMV, HIV, HSV, HTLV-1, JC (PML), VZV, West Nile
Parasitic
  • Cysticercosis, toxoplasma
Inflammatory/autoimmune
Acquired Inflammatory demyelinating diseases of the CNS
  • Acute hemorrhagic leukoencephalitis
  • ADEM
  • Aquaporin-4-IgG seropositive NMOSD
  • Aquaporin-4-IgG and MOG-IgG seronegative NMOSD
  • MS
  • Optic neuritis (single, recurrent, or CRION)
  • Transverse myelitis (single or recurrent)
Other inflammatory/autoimmune
  • Connective tissue disorder with CNS involvement (eg, lupus cerebritis, Neuro-Behcet, Sj√∂gren)
  • Neurosarcoidosis
  • Other idiopathic CNS disorders associated with antibodies (GABAA, GFAP, NMDA* etc)
  • Paraneoplastic disorders of the CNS with antibodies (eg, CRMP-5/anti-CV2)
Neoplastic
  • Glial neoplasms of brain or spinal cord (eg, astrocytoma, oligodendroglioma, ependymoma)
  • Histiocytic neoplasms (eg, Erdheim-Chester disease)
  • Lymphoma (primary CNS or systemic with CNS involvement)
  • Meningioma (particularly of optic nerve)
  • Metastatic disease rom solid organ tumor
Structural/other
  • Cervical spondylotic myelopathy
  • Fibrocartilaginous embolism
  • Hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis
  • Idiopathic intracranial hypertension/pseudotumor cerebri
  • Migraine
  • Tobacco-alcohol amblyopia
Toxic/metabolic
  • Nutritional deficiency (eg, B12, copper, folate, thiamine)
  • Biotinidase deficiency
  • Drug induced (eg, intrathecal methotrexate toxicity)
  • Radiation related (eg, radiation myelitis, SMART syndrome)
Vascular
  • CADASIL
  • Ischemia/infarct of spinal cord and optic nerve (brain infarcts easier to distinguish with DWI/ADC)
  • PRES
  • Primary or secondary CNS vasculitis including associated with infections (eg, VZV vasculopathy)
  • Susac syndrome
  • Vascular malformations (eg, dural arteriovenous fistula, cavernoma, telangiectasia)
