Survival advantage of arterial coronary bypass grafts
Estimated survival rates for patients with angina who underwent internal mammary artery (IMA, n = 749) or saphenous vein (n = 4888) coronary bypass grafts. Patient survival was significantly prolonged with IMA grafts (64 versus 53 percent at 15 years), a difference that increased over time.
Data from Cameron A, Davis KB, Green G, Schaff HV. N Engl J Med 1996; 334:216.
