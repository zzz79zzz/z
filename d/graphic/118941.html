Amanita muscaria - A. guessowii variant
The Amanita muscaria variant species A. guessowii can be found in the boreal forests of the northeast United States and in the northern Midwest. This photo was taken while mushroom foraging and hiking in the Adirondack Park in New York, USA. The guessowii variant of the Amanita muscaria species is distinguished by a yellow to orange cap instead of the reddish cap of the classic Amanita muscaria. These mushrooms contain the psychoactive chemicals ibotenic acid and muscimol and ingestion can result in hallucinations, confusion, sedation, nausea and vomiting among other clinical features. Seizures occasionally have been reported in pediatric ingestions.
