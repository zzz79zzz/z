Select tools for the diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder
Diagnostic instrument Age Description Comments
Autism Diagnostic Interview–Revised
  • Mental age ≥18 months
  • Two- to 3-hour clinical interview that probes for ASD symptoms
  • Not practical for clinical settings
  • Usually used in research settings, often combined with the ADOS-2
Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule–Second edition
  • Age 12 months through adulthood
  • Semistructured assessment by trained clinician of:
    • Social interaction
    • Play/imaginative use of materials
    • Communication
    • Atypical behaviors
  • Five modules based on child's expressive language abilities (including one for toddlers)
  • Takes 40 to 60 minutes to administer
  • Reference standard for diagnosis of ASD in research studies and in many clinical settings
Childhood Autism Rating Scale–Second edition (standard version)
  • Children ≥2 years of age
  • 15 items directly observed by a trained clinician
  • Can be completed by clinicians, parents, or teachers
  • Takes 20 to 30 minutes to administer
  • 15 items are correlated with DSM-IV
Developmental, Dimensional, and Diagnostic Interview
  • ≥3 years of age
  • Computerized parental interview
  • 266 questions related to ASD
  • 291 questions related to comorbid mental health conditions
  • Measures intensity of ASD symptoms and comorbidities
  • Takes 2 hours to administer
  • Predominantly used outside the United States
Diagnostic Interview for Social and Communication Disorder
  • Any age
  • Detailed semistructured interview with informant who knows patient well, ideally from infancy
  • Collects information on core symptoms of ASD and symptoms in other domains (eg, sensory, emotional, maladaptive behaviors)
  • Takes 3 hours to administer
  • Predominantly used outside the United States
Gilliam Autism Rating Scale–Third edition
  • Age 2 to 22 years
  • Checklist of 56 items based on DSM-5 criteria with subscales for:
    • Restrictive/repetitive behaviors
    • Social interaction
    • Social communication
    • Emotional responses
    • Cognitive style
    • Maladaptive speech
  • Completed by parents
  • Takes 5 to 10 minutes to administer
 
Instrument for higher-functioning children
Asperger Syndrome Diagnostic Scale
  • Age 5 to 18 years
  • 50 yes/no items in the following areas:
    • Cognitive
    • Maladaptive
    • Language
    • Social
    • Sensorimotor
  • Completed by parents, teachers, therapists, or other professionals
  • Takes 10 to 15 minutes to administer
 
Childhood Autism Rating Scale–Second edition (high-functioning version)
  • Children ≥2 years of age
  • 15 items directly observed by a trained clinician
  • Can be completed by clinicians, parents, or teachers
  • Takes 20 to 30 minutes to administer
  • 15 items are correlated with DSM-IV
These tools are recommended in national guidelines; they should be used in conjunction with clinical judgment. Their diagnostic accuracy has not been widely studied.
ASD: autism spectrum disorder; ADOS-2: Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule–Second edition; DSM-IV: Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders–Fourth edition; DSM-5: Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders–Fifth edition.
