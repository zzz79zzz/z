Treatment of rapidly progressing or progressively disfiguring cutaneous sarcoidosis
The approach to the treatment of less severe cutaneous sarcoidosis is reviewed in the UpToDate topic on the management of cutaneous sarcoidosis.
HCQ: hydroxychloroquine; MTX: methotrexate.
* The onset of action of these drugs is often delayed. We often continue prednisone early in the course of therapy until clinical improvement. Prednisone is subsequently tapered as tolerated. Similarly, HCQ or MTX are frequently continued while awaiting a response. The best course for patients who fail these agents is unclear. A variety of other therapies have been utilized for refractory cutaneous sarcoidosis, but efficacy data are limited. Refer to UpToDate topics on the management of cutaneous sarcoidosis for details on the use of these therapies and additional treatment options.
