M-mode of aortic root and valve in dilated cardiomyopathy
The M-mode echocardiogram from a normal subject (panel A) and a patient with dilated cardiomyopathy and low stroke volume (panel B) are shown. Note the difference in the cyclic anterior motion of the aortic root; the patient with low stroke volume has nearly flat root motion (panel B). Additionally, the aortic valve (Ao) has lost its "square wave" opening and now drifts closed as soon as it reaches its peak excursion. The valve appears more vivid in the low stroke volume patient partially because it moves less and more slowly, making it a better target for ultrasound. Also present is left atrial (LA) and right ventricular (RV) enlargement. The arrows point to reflected signals from a flow directed catheter in the patient's right ventricle outflow tract.
