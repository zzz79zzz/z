Chest radiograph showing cardiac resynchronization therapy: Defibrillator (CRT-D) and incidental central venous catheter
Chest radiography (anteroposterior view) showing a CIED with RV, RA, and coronary sinus leads. The shock coils identify this system as high-energy capable (implantable cardioverter-defibrillator). The coronary sinus lead is used to pace the LV for CRT. An incidental CVC is present.
CXR: chest radiograph; CIED: cardiac implantable cardioverter-defibrillator device; CRT: cardiac resynchronization therapy; CRT-D: cardiac resynchronization therapy-defibrillator; CVC: central venous catheter; LV: left ventricular/left ventricle; RV: right ventricular; RA: right atrial.
