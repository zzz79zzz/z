Natural history of hepatitis C virus
The likelihood of chronic infection following acquisition of HCV and the rate of fibrosis progression depend on various host and viral factors. As examples, young women and children are more likely to spontaneously clear HCV infection, and if chronically infected, have relatively slow fibrosis progression rates. Refer to the UpToDate topic on the natural history of HCV infection for further details.
