Hand compartment syndrome and fasciotomy
The images illustrate a case of compartment syndrome in a women in her sixties who presented with right hand swelling caused by a hematoma from anticoagulation (A: dorsal, B: palmar). Fasciotomy was performed and the hematoma was evacuated (C, D). She subsequently regained full range of finger motion three weeks later (E, F).
