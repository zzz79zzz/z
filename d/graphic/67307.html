Digital gangrene in Raynaud's
Patient with severe Raynaud's phenomenon and systemic lupus erythematosus who developed digital gangrene in the winter. She was treated conservatively, and the gangrenous digits self-amputated six months later. In patients with lupus, similar lesions can be seen with antiphospholipid antibodies.
