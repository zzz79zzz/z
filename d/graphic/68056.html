Recommended pre-exposure vaccination schedules for anthrax vaccine absorbed[1]
Timing Route Dose
Priming doses (3 doses):
  • 0 months
  • 1 month
  • 6 months
Intramuscular 0.5 mL
Initial booster doses (2 doses):
  • 12 months
  • 18 months
Ongoing booster doses:
  • For individuals with ongoing risk: Every year
  • For individuals who are not at high risk but want to maintain immunity: Every three years
Individuals may work in high-risk areas of exposure once they have completed the priming series. If an individual has completed the priming and initial booster doses but has not received a booster dose in the past year, prior to entering a high-risk area of inhalation exposure, he or she should receive an additional booster dose and delay entry until two weeks later; if immediate entry is required, antimicrobials should be given for two weeks following vaccination. Refer to other UpToDate content on antimicrobial use for post-exposure prophylaxis.
Reference:
  1. Bower WA, Schiffer J, Atmar RL, et al. Use of Anthrax Vaccine in the United States: Recommendations of the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices, 2019. MMWR Recomm Rep 2019; 68:1.
