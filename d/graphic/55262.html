Genotypic and phenotypic variants of dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa[1-3]
Subtype Inheritance
Mutated gene
Targeted gene product		 Clinical features
DDEB
DDEB, generalized
  • Autosomal dominant
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized blistering from birth, atrophic scarring, dystrophic (or absent) nails; alopecia
  • "Albopapuloid" lesions (small, grouped, hypopigmented papules usually on the lower back)
  • Involvement of oral cavity, gastrointestinal and, rarely, genitourinary tract
DDEB, acral
  • Autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Predilection of blistering for hands and feet from early infancy, milia, atrophic scarring, dystrophic (or absent) nails
DDEB, pretibial
  • Autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Pretibial blistering from birth or early infancy, involvement of hands and feet, milia, atrophic scarring; dystrophic nails
  • Lichen planus-like skin lesions
DDEB, pruriginosa
  • Autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized or localized erosions, blisters, milia, and atrophic scars from infancy or later; dystrophic nails
  • Pronounced pruritus
DDEB, nails only
  • Autosomal dominant
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Nail dystrophy or loss at birth or infancy without skin involvement
DDEB, bullous dermolysis of the newborn
  • Autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized blistering from birth or early infancy that improves dramatically over time
  • Milia, atrophic scarring; dystrophic nails
RDEB
RDEB, generalized severe (formerly recessive DEB-Hallopeau-Siemens)
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized blistering from birth, atrophic scarring, milia; sparse hair with scarring alopecia; dystrophic or absent nails
  • Acral pseudosyndactyly and contractures
  • Involvement of oral cavity (blisters, erosions, scarring, excessive caries, smooth tongue without papillae), gastrointestinal (protein losing enteropathy) and genitourinary tract (glomerulonephritis, IgA nephropathy, chronic renal failure), conjunctivae
  • Tremendous risk for development of squamous cell carcinomas
  • Cardiomyopathy, osteoporosis, anemia, impaired growth, delayed puberty
  • Shortened life expectancy
RDEB, generalized intermediate
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized blistering at birth, continue to develop throughout life; atrophic scarring, milia; nail dystrophy; scarring alopecia
  • Pseudosyndactyly
  • Increased risk for development of squamous cell carcinomas
  • Involvement of oral cavity, gastrointestinal and genitourinary tract, conjunctivae
  • Anemia, growth retardation
RDEB, inversa
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Blistering predominantly located in intertriginous lumbosacral, acral, and axial areas; milia, atrophic scarring; nail dystrophy;
  • Involvement of oral cavity (caries), gastrointestinal and genitourinary tract
  • Stenosis of meatus acusticus externus
  • Anemia, growth retardation
RDEB, pretibial
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Pretibial blistering at birth or early infancy, involvement of hands and feet; milia; atrophic scarring; nail dystrophy
  • Lichen planus-like skin lesions
RDEB, localized
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Blistering on hands and feet from early infancy, milia, atrophic scarring; nail dystrophy
RDEB, pruriginosa
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized or localized erosions, blisters, milia, and atrophic scars from infancy; dystrophic nails
  • Excessive pruritus
RDEB, centripetalis
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Pretibial and acral blistering at birth or from early infancy, milia, atrophic scarring; nail dystrophy
  • Involvement of oral mucosa
RDEB, bullous dermolysis of the newborn
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL7A1
  • Type VII collagen
  • Generalized blistering from birth or early infancy that improves dramatically over time
  • Milia, atrophic scarring; dystrophic nails
DDEB: dominant dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa; RDEB: recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa; IgA: immunoglobulin A.
