Improvement in bronchial responsiveness during pregnancy
Bronchial responsiveness, as measured by the dose of methacholine required to produce a 20 percent fall in FEV1 (PC20) before, during, and after pregnancy in 16 women with asthma. The PC20 increased 1.5 to 2-fold during pregnancy, indicating improved bronchial responsiveness.
Data from Juniper, EF, Daniel, EE, Roberts, RS, et al. Am Rev Respir Dis 1989; 140:924.
