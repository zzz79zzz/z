Herpes zoster ophthalmicus
The lesions of herpes zoster progress through stages, beginning as red macules and papules that, in the course of 7 to 10 days, evolve into vesicles and form pustules and crusts (scabs). A common site is the distribution of the ophthalmic division of the trigeminal nerve.
