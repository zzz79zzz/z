Protein load increases urine stone-forming tendency
The institution of a high protein diet (2 g/kg per day) in normal males adversely affects the metabolic parameters determining the risk of calcium stone formation. There is an increase in the urinary excretion of calcium and uric acid and a reduction in that of citrate.
Data from Kok, DJ, Iestra, JA, Doorenbos, CJ, Papapoulos, SE, J Clin Endocrinol Metab 1990; 71:861.
