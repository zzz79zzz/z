Graft versus native vessel disease as cause of unstable angina after CABG
Among patients with unstable angina who have had previous coronary artery bypass surgery (CABG), the older the saphenous vein graft, the greater the likelihood that the symptoms result from a culprit lesion within the graft (solid blue columns) rather than from a lesion in a native coronary artery (red columns).
%: percent.
Data from Chen L, Theroux P, Lesperance J, et al. J Am Coll Cardiol 1996; 28:1493.
