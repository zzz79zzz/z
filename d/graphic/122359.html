Metastatic calcification
Axial computed tomography (CT) scan (A) and coronal reformation (B) show conglomerate subpleural calcifications of the lung parenchyma in the right upper lobe and right lower lobe in a patient with chronic renal failure and high calcium phosphate product. These findings are consistent with metastatic calcification.
