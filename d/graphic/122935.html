Criteria used to decide to waken or proceed with surgery following failed tracheal intubation in a pregnant patient
  • Before induction of anesthesia, the anesthesia and obstetric teams should discuss whether to wake the patient or continue anesthesia in the event of a failed intubation. The factors to consider are shown in the top portion of this table, labeled preinduction planning. In particular, the anesthetist should consider whether it would be appropriate to use a supraglottic airway for the duration of surgery.
  • The bottom portion of the table shows the additional airway-related factors to consider after attempted intubation fails.
  • In any individual patient some factors may suggest wakening the patient and others may suggest proceeding with surgery. The final decision to waken or proceed will depend on the anesthetist's clinical judgement.
