Histopathologic examination of Aspergillus species
Special stains for histopathologic examination of aspergillus species (eg, periodic acid-Schiff or methenamine silver stain) demonstrate septate hyphae with characteristic acute angle branching (see arrows). Invasive disease is diagnosed when these hyphae are found to be eroding into tissue structures, in this case an ocular tumor.
