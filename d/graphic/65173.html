This table shows the proportion of patients with certain risk factors for chronic or acute recurrent pancreatitis from several different populations. Note that some patients may have more than one risk factor.
PRSS1: protease serine 1 gene; SPINK1: serine protease inhibitor Kazal-type 1 gene; CFTR: cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator gene; CTRC: chymotrypsin C gene; CPA1: carboxypeptidase A1.
* Data from the INSPPIRE cohort (United States, Canada, and Israel) of 301 children <19 years (mean age at onset of acute pancreatitis 8.1 years)[1].
¶ Data from a Japanese cohort of 128 children <16 years old (mean age at onset of acute pancreatitis 7.6 years)[2].
Δ Data from adults with non-alcoholic chronic pancreatitis from Germany (n = 944), France (n = 456), Czech Republic (n = 21), Poland (n = 123), India (n = 230), and Japan (n = 247)[3].
◊ Other obstructive/biliary risk factors include pancreatobiliary malunion, gallstones, trauma, sphincter of Oddi dysfunction, annular pancreas, and other duct obstructions.
§ Other toxic-metabolic includes active and passive tobacco smoking, alcohol use, chronic kidney disease, and organic acidemias.
