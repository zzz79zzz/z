Cystic changes in Langerhans cell histiocytosis
Langerhans cell pulmonary histiocytosis (eosinophilic granuloma) in an adolescent boy characterized by multiple cysts in the upper and lower lobes. The cysts are interspersed throughout the parenchyma and have a slightly thick wall. A posterior right upper lobe nodule and confluent bibasal patchy lower lobe opacities are also part of the disease process.
