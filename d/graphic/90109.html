Histologic mimics of cutaneous lymphoproliferative disorders
Mimickers of mycosis fungoides Features suggestive of reactive inflammatory process
Drug-induced pseudolymphoma Primarily clinical
Lichen sclerosis Location (genital), dense homogenized papillary dermal collagen
Lichen striatus Spongiosis, perieccrine inflammation
Lichenoid/lymphomatoid keratosis Wedge-shaped hypergranulosis, dyskeratotic keratinocytes, eosinophils, adjacent epithelial neoplasm (seborrheic keratosis, solar lentigo, etc)
Lymphomatoid contact dermatitis Spongiosis, papillary dermal edema, flask-shaped intraepidermal abscesses
Actinic reticuloid/chronic actinic dermatitis Spongiosis, epidermal acanthosis, CD8+ lymphocytes predominate
Vitiligo (inflammatory stage) Primarily clinical
Pigmented purpuric dermatitis Lack of cytologic atypia, lack of papillary dermal fibrosis
Fungal infection Neutrophils in stratum corneum, positive Periodic Acid-Schiff (PAS) stain
Arthropod assault Spongiosis, eosinophils, lymphoid follicles
Secondary syphilis Plasma cells, endarteritis, basal vacuolar interface changes
Mimickers of CD30+ lymphoproliferative disorders Features suggestive of reactive inflammatory process
Viral infections (Orf, Milker's nodule, molluscum contagiosum, herpes simplex) Scattered CD30+ cells, viropathic changes
Arthropod assault Scattered CD30+ cells, eosinophils, spongiosis
Mimickers of primary cutaneous B-cell lymphoma Features suggestive of reactive inflammatory process
Lymphomatoid drug reaction Primarily clinical
Persistent nodular arthropod bite reaction Epidermal spongiosis, eosinophils, endothelial swelling
Lymphocytoma cutis Well-formed germinal centers, "top-heavy" infiltrate, tingible body macrophages
Tattoo reaction Tattoo pigment
Vaccine reaction Central vertical focus of degeneration and fibrosis
