Consequences of smoking among adolescents with chronic illnesses
Chronic illness Potential disease-specific health consequences References
Asthma Increased frequency and severity of exacerbations, medication use, hospitalization, risk of respiratory arrest.

Larsson L. Thorax 1994; 50:260.

LeSon S. J Asthma 1995; 32:379.
Cystic fibrosis Increased frequency and severity of bacterial lung infection and hospitalization; accelerated decline in lung function, worse nutritional status.

Britto MT. Pediatrics 1998; 101:250.

Verma A. J R Soc Med 2001; 94:29.
Juvenile idiopathic (rheumatoid) arthritis Increased disease severity; increased risks for cardiovascular disease and premature death. Exacerbation of osteopenia.

Nash AA. Arthritis Care Res 1998; 11:391.

Gordon MM. Ann Rheum Dis 2003; 61:66.

French AR. J Rheumatol 2002; 29:1065.
Cancer Increased risks from several cancer treatments; respiratory infections when immunosuppressed; worse mucositis.

O'Driscoll BR. N Engl J Med 1990; 323:378.

Lipshultz SE. N Engl J Med 1991; 324.

Tyc VL. J Child Health Care 2004; 8:288.
Sickle cell disease Increased risk of acute chest syndrome; possible increased risk of stroke.

Young RC. J Natl Med Assoc 1992; 84:267.

Britto MT. Pediatrics 1998; 101:250.
Diabetes mellitus Accelerated cardiovascular and peripheral vascular disease, including atherosclerosis, retinopathy, and nephropathy. Smoking increases the morbidity and mortality of type 1 diabetes by 50 to 75%.

Frey MA. J Adolesc Health 1997; 20:38.

Cundiff DK. MedGenMed 2002; 4:2.

Gay EC. Diabetes Care 1992; 15:947.

Karim R. Diabet Med 2005; 22:81.
