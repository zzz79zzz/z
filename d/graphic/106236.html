Layers through which spinal needle goes
For spinal anesthesia, the needle is inserted through the skin and subcutaneous tissue, the supraspinous and interspinous ligaments, the ligamentum flavum, and the epidural space before puncturing the dura to enter the subarachnoid space. CSF flow from the needle hub confirms correct needle placement.
CSF: cerebrospinal fluid.
