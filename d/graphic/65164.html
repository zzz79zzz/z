Bronchiolitis obliterans following bone marrow transplantation
Photomicrographs of a completely obliterated bronchiole in a patient who has received an allogeneic bone marrow transplant. Left: Hematoxylin and eosin stained section shows complete replacement of a bronchiole by a collagenous scar associated with a scant inflammatory infiltrate and so-called cholesterol clefts, a nonspecific finding. Right: An elastic tissue stain is helpful in outlining the elastic layer of the same bronchiole pictured on the left and shows complete replacement of the bronchiole lumen by scar tissue.
