Disorders of peristalsis with esophageal spasticity or hypercontractility
Disorders of peristalsis with esophageal spasticity or hypercontractility. These include distal esophageal spasm or hypercontractile esophagus. In this example of distal esophageal spasm, the DCI is normal with a reduced distal latency and normal IRP. Hypercontractile esophagus includes subgroups: single peak hypercontractile swallow, hypercontractile with jackhammer esophagus, and hypercontractile with LES after contraction.
IRP: integrated relaxation pressure; DCI: distal contractile integral; LES: lower esophageal sphincter.
