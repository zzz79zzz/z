Chronic left lower lobe atelectasis
Chronic atelectasis of the left lower lobe due to previous pneumonia. A triangular opacity is faintly visible in the retrocardiac region (arrows). The left hilum is displaced caudad. The residual left lung is hyperlucent. A band of opacity, called the top of the knob sign, is present around the aortic arch (arrowhead); this finding is due to mediastinal shift to the left.
