* ALC day 1: absolute lymphocyte count per microL on day 1 after exposure.
¶ The lymphocyte fall rate constant is derived from a semilogarithmic plot of the absolute lymphocyte count (ALC) versus time in days, in the form of 2450 x e–kt. The time (in days) for the ALC to fall to one-half of its original value [half-time, T(1/2)] can be obtained from the following equation: T(1/2) = 0.693/k.
Δ Dicentric chromosomes are those containing two separate centromeres, caused by end-to-end recombination of two damaged chromosomes.
