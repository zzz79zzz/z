Carpal tunnel syndrome
The carpal tunnel is a tunnel in the wrist that is formed by the bones of the wrist and a tough band of tissue called a "ligament." Carpal tunnel syndrome happens when a nerve that goes through that tunnel, called the "median nerve," gets pinched or squeezed. Carpal tunnel syndrome causes pain and numbness most often in the areas shaded here in blue.
