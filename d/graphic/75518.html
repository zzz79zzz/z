Unstable comminuted distal phalanx shaft fracture with nail bed laceration
Shaft fractures are usually stable and can be managed with a simple splint that immobilizes the distal interphalangeal joint (DIPJ) but leaves the proximal interphalangeal joint (PIPJ) free. However, comminution of the distal phalanx shaft can render the fracture unstable. In such cases, open reduction with internal fixation (ORIF) is needed.
