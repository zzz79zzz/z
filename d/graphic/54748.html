Vertical growth phase malignant melanoma
Low power photomicrographs of a nodular, vertical growth phase melanoma characterized by the formation of an expansile nodule of melanoma cells in the dermis. Clinically, this results in a papule or nodule arising against the background of the flat radial growth phase.
(A) The invasive melanoma has a polypoid growth pattern arising in a superficial spreading melanoma. The surface is ulcerated, and the maximum depth of invasion is measured from the base of the ulcer to the deepest melanoma cell.
(B) The invasive melanoma is forming large tumor nodules and has invaded the subcutis (arrows).
