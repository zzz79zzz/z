Diagnostic ultrasound of ITB syndrome
These ultrasound images of the right knee were obtained before (A and C) and after (B and D) the patient went on a training run. The ITB (indicated by hashmarks) increased in thickness from 1.9 mm before the run (A) to 2.5 mm after the run (B). Color-powered Doppler of the ITB itself ("X") does not show increased vascularity, but the adipose tissue ("a") deep to the ITB shows increased vascularity (D) after the training run.
ITB: liotibial band.
Blue circle: proximal aspect of image; green square: lateral femoral condyle.
From: Abdelshahed D, Neuman S, Oh-Park M. Dynamic Change in Ultrasonographic findings in iliotibial band syndrome after running. Am J Phys Med Rehabil 2018; 97:e13. DOI: 10.1097/PHM.0000000000000760.
