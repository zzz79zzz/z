External rotation technique for reduction of anterior shoulder dislocation
With the patient's arm adducted and the elbow flexed, the forearm is slowly and gently externally rotated. If pain or spasm is felt, the physician stops and allows the patient to relax. No longitudinal traction is necessary. In most cases, by the time the shoulder is fully externally rotated, the shoulder will have been reduced.
