* For patients at low risk for fracture in the near future (eg, stable bone density, no previous vertebral or hip fractures), we suggest discontinuing therapy to reduce potential risks of long-term therapy, as there appears to be residual fracture benefit post-treatment.
¶ Most oral bisphosphonates should be taken alone on an empty stomach first thing in the morning with at least 240 mL (8 oz) of water. After administration, the patient should not have food, drink, medications, or supplements and should remain upright for at least one half-hour (alendronate, risedronate) or one hour (ibandronate).
Δ The 70 mg dose is also available in an effervescent formulation (dissolved over at least 5 minutes in 4 oz of tap water) and an oral solution (follow administration with at least 2 oz of water).
◊ Based on indirect evidence from the alendronate extension trial.
§ The enteric-coated, delayed-release formulation is taken immediately after breakfast with 4 oz of water.
