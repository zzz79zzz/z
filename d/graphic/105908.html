Histopathology of autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease
Kidney histopathologic findings in a patient with autosomal dominant polycystic disease include the characteristic cortical and medullary cysts of varying sizes that are lined with cuboidal or flattened epithelium, tubular atrophy, interstitial fibrosis, and lymphocytic infiltrates.
