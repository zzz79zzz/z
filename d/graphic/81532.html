Differential diagnosis of mesenteric masses
CT appearance Pathologic conditions Associated findings
Round, well-defined mass(es) Non-Hodgkin lymphoma Retroperitoneal adenopathy
Metastic deposits Small bowel nodules
Mycobacterium avium complex infection Hepatic and splenic lesions
Whipple's disease Thickened small bowel folds
Mesenteric cysts Fat attenuation
Castelman disease Hypervascular mass
Ill-defined, irregular mass(es) Small bowel carcinoid Calcifications; small bowel mass
Mesenteric fibromatosis (desmoid) Gardner's syndrome; abdominal wall infiltration
Tumor infiltration (pancreas, gastric, colon) Primary tumor
Lymphoma Retroperitoneal adenopathy
Mesenteric panniculitis/sclerosing mesenteritis Calcifications
Stellate mesentery Carcinomatosis Ascites
Peritoneal lymphomatosis Adenopathy
Peritoneal mesothelioma Ascites; pleuroperitoneal plaques
Peritoneal tuberculosis Ileocolonic thickening; ascites; peritoneal enhancement
Systemic amyloidosis Thrombosis; focal mesenteric thickening
Vascular thrombosis
