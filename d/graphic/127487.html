Male sling
A male sling is a procedure where mesh is placed under the urethra to either relocate the proximal urethra and/or compress the bulbous urethra. Male slings are recommended for nonradiated patients who have mild-to-moderate stress urinary incontinence after prostate surgery, and success rates are highest in men with pad weights <200 g per day.
Modified from: Male slings for urinary incontinence: advance sling. CAMC Health System. Available at: https://www.camc.org/male-slings-for-urinary-incontinence (Accessed on March 23, 2020).
