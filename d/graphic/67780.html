Characteristic eruption of Gianotti-Crosti syndrome (papular acrodermatitis)
The characteristic rash of Gianotti-Crosti syndrome consists of symmetric, pink-brown, papular or papulovesicular lesions that are flat-topped and range from 1 to 10 mm in diameter. The face, buttocks, extensor aspects of forearms and legs, and feet are predominantly affected.
