External landmarks for access to the femoral vein
The midinguinal point lying half-way between the anterior superior iliac spine and the symphysis pubis marks the normal location of the femoral artery. The femoral vein lies just medial to the artery. Remember that pulsations will be felt in the femoral vein in cardiac arrest patients receiving chest compressions.
