Location of mutations in the thyroid hormone receptor beta molecule causing resistance to thyroid hormone
(A) Schematic representation of the TR-beta molecule and its functional domains for interaction with TREs (DNA-binding) and with hormone (T3-binding). Their relationship to the 3 clusters of natural mutations is also indicated. TR-beta-2 has 15 more residues than TR-beta-1 at the amino terminus.
(B) The location of the 236 different mutations detected and their frequencies in the total of 805 unrelated families (published and our unpublished data). Amino acids are numbered consecutively, starting at the amino terminus of the TR-beta-1 molecule, according to the consensus statement of the First International Workshop on RTH. "Cold regions" are areas devoid of mutations associated with RTH-beta.
TR-beta: thyroid hormone receptor beta; T3: 3,3',5-triiodothyronine; TREs: thyroid hormone response elements; RTH: resistance to thyroid hormone.
