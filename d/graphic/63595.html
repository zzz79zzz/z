Squamous papilloma
A typical squamous papilloma, which is surfaced by bland and maturing squamous cells around a core of loose fibrovascular tissue. This exophytic lesion is usually solitary and often human papillomavirus (HPV)-related. Multiple lesions characterize recurrent respiratory papillomatosis (RRP) usually seen in the larynx.
