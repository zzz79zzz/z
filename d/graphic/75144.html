Bilateral adrenal hyperplasia
T1-weighted MRI through the upper abdomen demonstrates enlargement of both adrenal glands (arrows). This finding could be caused by ACTH-dependent Cushing's syndrome or primary hyperaldosteronism due to adrenal hyperplasia.
ACTH: corticotropin; MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
