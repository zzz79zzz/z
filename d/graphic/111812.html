Bimanual laryngoscopy in a child
While performing direct laryngoscopy, the laryngoscopist uses the right hand to sweep the thyroid cartilage to the right and upward until the optimal glottis view is obtained. At that point, the laryngoscopist instructs an assistant to place their fingers in the exact same spot and apply the same direction and degree of force so that the endotracheal tube can be placed.
