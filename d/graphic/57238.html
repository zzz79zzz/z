Typical electroanatomic map of infarcted myocardium
The electroanatomic map of a patient with a myocardial infarction shows large regions of abnormal electromechanical activity and reduced local shortening (red, abnormally contracting regions with values <4 percent). In contrast, a region in which function remains unhindered is a color-coded blue/purple, normal contractile function >12 percent.
