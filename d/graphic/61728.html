Echocardiogram of Ebstein anomaly
Two-dimensional echocardiogram in the apical four-chamber view (panel A) from a patient with Ebstein anomaly shows a displaced, long ("sail"-like) anterior leaflet of the tricuspid valve, which is attached to the tricuspid annulus (dashed arrow) and apical displacement of the septal leaflet (arrowhead). Note the large right atrialized (RA) portion of the right ventricle (RV). Color Doppler echocardiogram (panel B) shows severe tricuspid regurgitation originating at the level of the displaced tricuspid valve.
