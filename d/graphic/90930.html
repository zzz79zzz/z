Selected risk factors associated with acquisition of Serratia infection during hospital outbreaks
Adult outbreaks[1-3] 
Receipt of contaminated medication
Indwelling device (central venous catheter, urinary catheter)
Endotracheal intubation
Longer hospital or intensive care unit stay
Antibiotic exposure prior to infection
Tube feeding
Parenteral nutrition
Medical comorbidities
Immune compromise
Neonatal outbreaks[4,5] 
Low birth weight
Maternal infection prior to delivery (eg, chorioamnionitis)
Longer hospital or intensive care unit stay
Antibiotic exposure prior to infection
Central venous catheter
Endotracheal intubation
