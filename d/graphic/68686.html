Impact of age on adverse cerebral outcomes after coronary bypass surgery
The incidence of adverse cerebral events after coronary artery bypass surgery is 6.1 percent, of which half are type I (focal injury, coma, or stupor at discharge) and half are type II (deterioration in intellectual function, memory defect, or seizures). The incidence of both type I and II events increase with age.
Data from Roach GW, Kanchuger M, Mangano M, et al, and the Ischemia Research and Education Foundation Investigators. N Engl J Med 1996; 335:1857.
