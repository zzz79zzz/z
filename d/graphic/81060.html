Uncomplicated local reaction to Hymenoptera sting
Uncomplicated local reactions from the sting of Hymenoptera insects usually consist of erythema and an area of painful swelling 1 to 2 cm in diameter surrounding the puncture site from the stinger. These reactions typically subside within a few hours.
