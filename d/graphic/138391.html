Common nicotine-containing products: Estimated concentration and toxic amount after ingestion in children
Nicotine source Concentration* Toxic amount
E-cigarette solution 0.5 to 36 mg/mL Ingestion or oral exposure to any amount¶
Nicotine dermal patch 8 to 114 mg per patch Any swallowed portion¶
Cigarette 7 to 15 mg per cigarette ≥3 cigarette butts; ≥1 cigarette
Cigar 7 to 40 mg 1 cigar butt
Gum 2 or 4 mg per stick ½ to 1 stick (2 mg) or ¼ to ½ stick (4 mg)
Snuff (wet) 12 to 16 mg/g 0.125 g (½ small pinch)
Chewing tobacco 6 to 8 mg/g 0.25 g (small pinch)
Orb (flavored) 1 mg per pellet 1 to 2 orbs
Snus (flavored tobacco pouch) 4 to 16 mg/gΔ ½ pouch
Snus (loose) 11 to 27 mg/gΔ 0.125 g (½ small pinch)

* Precise nicotine content varies by brand.

¶ Any dermal exposure to concentrated e-cigarette solution or longer than 20 minutes to a transdermal nicotine patch can result in serious toxicity.

Δ Between 20 to 40% of nicotine is extracted during normal oral use depending upon the specific product.
