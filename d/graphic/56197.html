LVH with repolarization abnormalities
Precordial leads in a hypertensive patient with LVH. The voltage far exceeds commonly used voltage criteria for LVH in adults greater than 35 years old: the S in V1 plus the R in V5 equals 50 mm (upper limit of normal 35 mm). Voltage criteria alone may not be diagnostic of LVH. However, the associated repolarization abnormality (formerly referred to as a "strain" pattern) in leads V5 and V6, characterized by downward sloping ST-segment depression and T-wave inversion, make LVH highly likely. Left atrial abnormality consistent with enlargement is also present as shown by the biphasic P wave with a broad negative deflection in lead V1. These changes can be induced by a chronic pressure load (hypertension or aortic stenosis) or a chronic volume load (aortic or mitral regurgitation or dilated cardiomyopathy).
