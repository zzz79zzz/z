Surgical excision of digital keloids

(A) View before surgery.

(B) Core excision design.

(C) Immediately after excision of the keloid cores.

(D) Immediately after closure.

(E, F) Eighteen months after surgery.

A 30-year-old female patient developed keloids on 3 toes after surgery for ingrown nails. The keloid on the second toe had extended around the whole toe past the distal interphalangeal joint. A pen was used to delineate the flaps over the keloid cores that would be used to close the wounds. The flap skin was selected on the basis of local blood perfusion. Core excision surgery was conducted, and the flaps were trimmed and stitched. Recurrence was not observed. After 18 months, the toes had a satisfactory appearance.
