Cervical root dental fracture
This radiograph shows a cervical root fracture in a permanent tooth (arrows). This injury requires splinting of the crown of the tooth to prevent mobility and permit healing. However, if the crown remains mobile after splinting, then crown removal followed by extrusion, root canal, and restoration is usually necessary.
