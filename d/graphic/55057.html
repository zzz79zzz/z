MRI of cervical spondylitic myelopathy
Cervical spondylitic myelopathy. Sagittal T2-weighted MRI of the cervical spine shows spinal canal narrowing due to disc-osteophyte complexes indenting the ventral aspect of the spinal cord (arrows) and abnormal T2 hyperintense cord signal (arrowhead) confirming the diagnosis of myelopathy.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
