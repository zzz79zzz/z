Total number of mutant genes in table: 45. CVID disorders include several clinical and laboratory phenotypes that may be caused by distinct genetic and/or environmental factors. Some patients with CVID and no known genetic defect have markedly reduced numbers of B cells as well as hypogammaglobulinemia. Identification of causal variants can assist in defining treatment. In addition to monogenic causes on this table, a small minority of patients with X-linked lymphoproliferative disease, WHIM syndrome, ICF, VODI, thymoma with immunodeficiency (Good syndrome), or myelodysplasia are first seen by an immunologist because of recurrent infections, hypogammaglobulinemia, and normal or reduced number of B cells.
