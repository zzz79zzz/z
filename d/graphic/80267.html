Plain chest radiograph of HAPE
Plain chest radiography is useful in HAPE and reveals characteristic patchy alveolar infiltrates, predominantly in the right central hemithorax, which become more confluent and bilateral as the illness progresses. In a few cases, HAPE may not take this classic radiographic appearance.
HAPE: High altitude pulmonary edema.
