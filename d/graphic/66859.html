Closure using Pelosi technique
(A) Exteriorization of the uterus through the abdominal incision is easily accomplished.
(B) Following completion of the hysterotomy closure, the self-retaining retractor is removed.
(C) The visceral and parietal peritoneum are not closed.
(D) No suture approximation of the rectus muscles is performed. The fascia is closed in a continuous, non-locking fashion with delayed-absorbable sutures placed at least 1 cm from the fascial wound edge (arrows).
(E) The subcutaneous tissue is not closed except when this layer is ≥2 cm thick. The skin is closed with subcuticular sutures or (F) metal staples.
