Placenta increta on MRI
Sagittal T2-weighted images through the placenta (A) show an abnormal uterine contour (arrow) with discontinuity of the hypointense inner myometrial layer (arrowhead). T2 low-signal placental bands are also noted (arrowhead). Image B is a magnified view of image A and shows the normal hypointense myometrium (arrowhead) with discontinuity of the muscle layer and an abnormal uterine contour (arrow). The outer layer of the myometrium is intact. There are thick and irregular placental bands (dashed arrow). Placenta increta was documented at delivery. Axial T2 images through the placenta demonstrate T2 low-signal placental bands, which are irregular and thicker than normal placental septae.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
