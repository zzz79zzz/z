Clinical grading of vernal keratoconjunctivitis obtained from the classification tree analysis
Grade Clinical findings Treatment
0 quiescent Absence of symptoms No treatment
1 mild Presence of symptoms, but no corneal involvement Antiallergic eye drops occasionally
2 moderate Presence of symptoms including photophobia, but no corneal involvement Antiallergic eye drops daily
3 severe Presence of symptoms including photophobia, plus mild to moderate SPK Antiallergic eye drops daily and pulsed low-dose topical corticosteroid
4 very severe Presence of symptoms including photophobia, plus diffuse SPK or corneal ulcer Pulsed high-dose topical corticosteroid and eventual surgical removal of corneal plaque
SPK: superficial punctuate keratopathy.
Reproduced from: Sacchetti M, Lambiase A, Mantelli F, et al. Tailored approach to the treatment of vernal keratoconjunctivitis. Ophthalmology 2010; 117:1294. Table used with the permission of Elsevier, Inc. All rights reserved.
