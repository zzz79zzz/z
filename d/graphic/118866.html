NEXUS guide to imaging blunt chest trauma
NEXUS Chest:
  1. age >60;
  2. rapid deceleration mechanism (fall >6 meters [20 feet] or motorized vehicle accident >65 km [40 miles] per hour);
  3. chest pain;
  4. intoxication;
  5. distracting injury;
  6. tenderness to chest wall palpation; and
  7. abnormal alertness/mental status.
NEXUS CT Major:
  1. distracting injury;
  2. chest wall tenderness;
  3. sternal tenderness;
  4. thoracic spine tenderness; and
  5. scapular tenderness.
NEXUS CT All:
  1. CT Major criteria plus rapid deceleration mechanism.
NEXUS: National Emergency X-radiography Utilization Studies; CT: computed tomography.
