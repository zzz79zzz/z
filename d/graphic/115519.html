Iris transillumination
Slit lamp photomicrograph showing punctate iris transillumination in a patient with OCA1B. The light is reflected from the retina and choroid back through punctate defects in pigment in the posterior iris epithelium.
OCA1B: oculocutaneous albinism type 1B.
Reproduced from: Summers CG, Knobloch WH, Witkop CJ Jr, King RA. Hermansky-Pudlak syndrome: Ophthalmic findings. Ophthalmology 1988; 95:545. Illustration used with the permission of Elsevier Inc. All rights reserved.
