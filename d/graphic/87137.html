Cecal bascule – CT scan
A multiplanar CT examination demonstrates a cecal bascule (B and C, arrow). Note the normal location of the ascending colon (A, arrow) and the region of fold with subsequent migration of the cecum in an anterior and cephalad direction (C, arrowhead). There is no evidence of vascular engorgement nor pericecal inflammatory change.
CT: computed tomography.
