Approval of these GnRH agonists for use in central precocious puberty, dosing, and schedules of administration vary between products in different countries. Consult local product information. Short-acting GnRH preparations requiring daily subcutaneous injections or multiple daily dosing nasal spray (such as nafarelin) are not recommended, due to compliance difficulty; use of these products usually is limited to patients with sterile abscesses from depot injections. Note that the doses used to treat central precocious puberty in children are substantially higher than those used in adults for other indications, such as prostate cancer and endometriosis.
