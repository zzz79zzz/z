Type III hiatal hernia
Upper GI (gastrointestinal) series showing a type III hiatal hernia. Type III hernias have elements of both types I and II. With progressive enlargement of the hernia through the hiatus, the phrenoesophageal membrane stretches, displacing the gastroesophageal junction above the diaphragm, thereby adding a sliding element to the type II hernia.
Reprinted with permission from: Peridikis G, Hinder RA. Paraesophageal hiatal hernia. In: Hernia, Nyhus LM, Condon RE (Eds), JB Lippincott, Philadelphia 1995. p.544.
