Determining the cause of a water diuresis in adults and adolescents with polyuria and normal serum sodium using water restriction and desmopressin

AVP-R: arginine vasopressin resistance (previously called nephrogenic diabetes insipidus); AVP-D: arginine vasopressin deficiency (previously called central diabetes insipidus); IV: intravenously; ADH: antidiuretic hormone.

* If primary polydipsia is strongly suspected, then aqueous vasopressin (10 units intramuscularly or subcutaneously) should be given instead of desmopressin.
