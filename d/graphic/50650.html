Bone marrow megakaryocyte
High power view (x1000) of a Wright-stained bone marrow. A large normal megakaryocyte is seen with a polyploid nucleus within one nuclear envelope. The staining characteristics of the cytoplasm are identical to those of the platelet. Adjacent cells include neutrophils, erythrocytes, and some myeloid precursors.
