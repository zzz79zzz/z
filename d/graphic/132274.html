Intracerebral hemorrhage due to cerebral sinus thrombosis
Noncontrast head CT showing left frontal ICH (arrow) and vasogenic edema (arrowhead) (A). Mid-sagittal plane on CT angiography (B) and MRV (C) showing filling defect (thick arrows) in superior sagittal sinus (B). Subsequent brain MRI with FLAIR (D) and T2* gradient recall echo (E) imaging showing larger ICH (arrows) and associated edema (arrowheads).
ICH: intracerebral hemorrhage; CT: computed tomography; MRV: magnetic resonance venography; MRI: magnetic resonance imaging; FLAIR: fluid-attenuated inversion recovery.
