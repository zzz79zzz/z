The Khorana score was developed from a cohort of 2701 patients with cancer receiving a first course of chemotherapy to predict the cumulative incidence of VTE at 2.5 months[1]. The score was validated in a cohort of 1365 patients, most of whom (91%) had an ECOG performance status of 0 or 1. The score was tested independently in 819 patients with newly diagnosed cancer or cancer progression who had not received chemotherapy within the preceding 3 months to predict the cumulative incidence of VTE at 6 months[2], and in 1415 patients enrolled in phase I clinical trials followed for a median of 2 months[3].
ESAs: erythropoiesis-stimulating agents; WBC: white blood cell; BMI: body mass index; VTE: venous thromboembolism; ECOG: Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group.
