Empyema with split pleura sign
CT scan demonstrates a loculated collection of pleural effusion in the left hemithorax with enhancing visceral and parietal pleura ("split pleura" sign) and a few bubbles of gas within the empyema, likely due to a gas forming organism. The left lower atelectatic lung is compressed against the mediastinum. Larger collections of gas are usually indicative of a bronchopleural fistula. The contralateral right transudative effusion does not show enhancement of the pleura after intravenous contrast material administration (Contarini's condition).
CT: computed tomography.
