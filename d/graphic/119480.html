MRI in reversible posterior leukoencephalopathy syndrome (RPLS)
(A) MRI was obtained one day following CT, and T2-weighted image shows hyperintensity in the white matter of the occipital lobes.
(B) FLAIR image obtained more rostrally shows multifocal areas of hyperintensity both posteriorly and anteriorly; note that on the FLAIR image, the hyperintensity involves the gray as well as the white matter.
(C) DWI shows slight hyperintensity within the hyperintense regions seen on FLAIR image.
​(D) The corresponding ADC map shows hyperintensity in these regions, suggestive of facilitated water diffusion and vasogenic edema.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging; CT: computed tomography; FLAIR: fluid-attenuated inversion recovery; DWI: diffusion-weighted imaging; ADC: apparent diffusion coefficient.
