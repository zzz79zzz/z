Clinical syndromes associated with bronchiolitis

Clinical syndromes associated with bronchiolitis
Inhalation injury Associated diseases
  • Toxic fume inhalation (eg, silo fillers, welders, mustard gas)
  • Mineral dusts
  • Irritant gases (eg, chlorine, ammonia, paraquat)
  • Volatile flavoring agents (popcorn factory)
  • Electronic cigarettes ("vaping" or "dabbing")
  • Free-base cocaine
  • Organ transplantation
    • Bone marrow
    • Heart-lung
    • Lung
  • Rheumatic disease
    • Rheumatoid arthritis
    • Sjögren syndrome
    • Systemic lupus erythematosus
    • Polymyositis/dermatomyositis
  • Rare associations
    • Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS)
    • Ataxia telangiectasia
    • Lysinuric protein intolerance
    • Malignant histiocytosis
    • Paraneoplastic pemphigus
    • Primary biliary cholangitis
    • Ulcerative colitis
    • Vasculitis, especially granulomatosis with polyangiitis
Postinfectious
  • Viruses
    • Respiratory syncytial virus
    • Adenovirus or rhinovirus
    • Influenza or parainfluenza
    • Measles or mumps
    • Varicella zoster
    • Cytomegalovirus
    • Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV)
  • Other infectious agents
    • Bordetella pertussis
    • Mycoplasma pneumoniae
    • Nocardia
Drug-induced reactions Idiopathic
  • Busulfan
  • Gold
  • Nimesulide
  • Penicillamine
  • Rituximab
  • Sauropus androgynus leaf (papaverine)
  • Sulfasalazine
  • Sulfamethoxypyridazine
  • Cryptogenic bronchiolitis obliterans
References:

  2. Chatté G, Streichenberger N, Boillot O. Lymphocytic bronchitis/bronchiolitis in a patient with primary biliary cirrhosis. Eur Respir J 1995; 8:176.
