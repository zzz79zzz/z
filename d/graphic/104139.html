Plain radiographs of cam morphology femoroacetabular impingement
(A) This anteroposterior (AP) plain radiograph of the right hip of a 39-year-old woman shows excessive bone buildup at the femoral head-neck junction (arrow). There are signs of secondary osteoarthritis of the hip joint (eg, narrowed joint space; sclerosis).
(B) This AP hip radiograph of a 41-year-old man demonstrates the tubular appearance of the proximal right femur and the osseous prominence at the femoral head-neck junction. Also evident is osteoarthritis of the hip joint.
