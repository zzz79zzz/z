Optimal INR to minimize both bleeding and thromboembolism in patients with atrial fibrillation
(A) ORs for TE (396 cases, 1581 controls) and ICH (164 cases, 656 controls) by INR level in adults with nonvalvular AF, with 8 INR categories using INR 2.0 to 2.5 as the referent. Vertical bars indicate 95% CI. The numbers of cases and controls for each INR category are given below the figure.
(B) ORs for TE (396 cases, 1581 controls) and ICH (164 cases, 656 controls) by INR level in adults with nonvalvular AF, with 6 INR categories using INR 2.0 to 2.5 as the referent. Vertical bars indicate 95% CI. The numbers of cases and controls for each INR category are given below the figure.

AF: atrial fibrillation; INR: international normalized ratio; OR: odds ratio; TE: thromboembolism; ICH: intracranial hemorrhage; CI: confidence interval.
