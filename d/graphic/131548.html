Trisomy 21 with hydrops
(A) Midsagittal view of the fetal profile at 20 weeks in a fetus with trisomy 21 showing extensive skin thickening along the forehead (arrow). This fetus was the presenting twin of a dichorionic-diamniotic twin pair (membrane; dashed arrow) with a euploid non-presenting twin.
(B) Axial scan through the fetal head showing the extreme skin thickening (bars) circumferentially around the cranium.
(C) View through the fetal face showing the orbits (short arrows) and the extreme skin thickening around the lateral aspect of the fetal face (bars).
(D) Axial scan through the fetal chest showing extreme skin thickening (bar) and pleural effusions (arrowheads), dividing membrane (dashed arrow). This fetus also had ascites (not shown).
T21: trisomy 21.
