Principal features and cardiovascular/pericardial involvement in vasculitides
Vasculitis Principal features Estimated frequency of cardiac/pericardial involvement (%) Type of cardiac/pericardial involvement
Takayasu arteritis Chronic inflammatory and obliterative disease of the aorta and its major branches Cardiac: rare Fibrosis, narrowing and aneurysm of the aorta, its branches, and pulmonary arteries
Pericardial: rare (case reports)  
Giant cell arteritis Vasculitis that affects large and medium-sized blood vessels (especially cranial arteries branching from the carotid artery) Cardiac: <10 Aneurysm, coronary arteritis, myocardial infarction
Pericardial: rare (case reports) Pericarditis, pericardial effusion
Polyarteritis nodosa Necrotizing arteritis involving medium-sized and small-sized arteries with inflammation and ischemia of affected organs Cardiac: <50 Hypertension (renal vasculitis), aneurysm, and myocardial infarction
Pericardial: rare (case reports, limited series) Pericarditis
Kawasaki disease Systemic necrotizing medium-sized vessel vasculitis mainly affecting blood vessels, skin, mucous membranes, and lymph nodes Cardiac: 30 Coronary arteritis and aneurysm, myocardial infarction, myocarditis, heart failure
Pericardial: 30 Pericardial effusion, pericarditis
Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Churg-Strauss) Systemic necrotizing vasculitis affecting small-sized to medium-sized vessels, characterized by asthma, hypereosinophilia, and eosinophil-rich granulomatous inflammation of the respiratory tract Cardiac: >50 Eosinophilic endomyocarditis, heart failure, conduction system disease
Pericardial: 25 to 50 Pericarditis, pericardial effusion
Granulomatosis with polyangiitis Necrotizing vasculitis that mainly affects arteries in the respiratory tract and kidneys Cardiac: 10 to 40 Coronary arteritis, valve lesions, conduction system disease, aortic aneurysm
Pericardial: <10 Pericarditis, pericardial effusion
Behçet syndrome Systemic necrotizing vasculitis involving small-, medium-, and large-sized vessels, characterized by recurrent aphthous stomatitis, genital ulcerations, and uveitis Cardiac: <10 Endocarditis, aortic regurgitation, intracardiac thrombosis, myocardial infarction, endomyocardial fibrosis, myocardial aneurysm
Pericardial: ≤10 Pericarditis
