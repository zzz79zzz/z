Prenatal ultrasound of a congenital pulmonary airway malformation (CPAM)*
After measuring the longest dimension of the lesion (L), the probe is turned 90 degrees and the "width" (W) and "height" (H) of the lesion are measured. The L x W x H x 0.52 = volume of CPAM. The CPAM volume ratio (CVR) is then calculated by dividing the CPAM volume by the head circumference.
* CPAM was previously known as congenital cystic adenomatoid malformation (CCAM).
