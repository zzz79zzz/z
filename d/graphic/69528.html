Lymph nodes of the head and neck
This drawing schematically depicts the major lymph nodes in the head and neck area that are likely to be enlarged on physical examination in patients with various local or systemic diseases. The major nodal groups are shown here in bold, with the areas draining into these nodal groups noted when appropriate. While enlargement of both the left and right supraclavicular lymph nodes may reflect disease in the thorax, left supraclavicular nodal enlargement, because of its drainage pattern, may also reflect the presence of abdominal involvement (ie, Virchow node).
