Moderate alcohol intake reduces mortality
In a middle-aged and older adult population study of 490,000 subjects, all-cause mortality and cardiovascular mortality were reduced with moderate alcohol consumption. However, mortality increased with intake greater than 2 drinks per day due to death from alcohol-related conditions and cancer.
Data from Thun MJ, Peto R, Lopez AD, et al. N Engl J Med 1997; 337:1705.
