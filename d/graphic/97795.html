Hemorrhagic HCC treated with embolization
An angiogram of a hemorrhagic HCC (A) shows a hypervascular mass in the dome of the liver (arrow) with a central region of hypovascularity (arrowhead) likely representing intratumoral clot or necrosis. Image B is an angiogram of the right hepatic artery following microsphere embolization and shows an avascular mass (arrow).
HCC: hepatocellular carcinoma.
