Clinical spectrum of fusariosis

Clinical spectrum of fusariosis
Immunocompetent hosts
Non-infectious
  • Mycotoxicosis
  • Allergic bronchopulmonary fusariosis
Infectious
Common:
  • Onychomycosis
  • Keratitis*
Uncommon:
  • Cutaneous (burns, wounds)[1]
  • Peritonitis (patients receiving CAPD)[2-4]
  • Endocarditis
  • Pneumonia
  • Sinusitis
  • Endophthalmitis
  • Thrombophlebitis
  • Fungemia
  • Arthritis
  • CNS infection
Immunocompromised hosts[5]
Invasive infection
Disseminated
  • Multiorgan infection
  • Isolated fungemia
Localized
Common:
  • Cellulitis
  • Sinusitis
  • Pneumonia
Less common:
  • CNS infection
  • Endophthalmitis
  • Arthritis/osteomyelitis

CAPD: continuous ambulatory peritoneal dialysis; CNS: central nervous system.

* Outbreaks of fusarial keratitis have been reported among contact lens wearers[6].
