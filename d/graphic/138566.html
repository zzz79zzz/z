Chest wall keloid treated by surgical excision and postoperative radiation therapy

A 70-year-old male patient developed chest wall keloids after cardiovascular surgery. The keloids were excised completely, and subcutaneous/fascial tensile reduction sutures were placed to close the wound. The wound was then subjected to 18 Gy/3 fractions/3 days of electron beam radiotherapy. The inflammation decreased without complications. The scar was mature by 18 months.
