Diabetics with a non-ST elevation ACS have a worse outcome than nondiabetics
In the OASIS registry of 8013 patients with a non-ST elevation acute coronary syndrome (unstable angina or non Q-wave myocardial infarction), 21 percent had diabetes. After a two-year follow-up, diabetic patients had a significantly higher combined event rate (cardiovascular death, new myocardial infarction, stroke, new heart failure) than nondiabetics (relative risk 1.56).
Data from Malmberg K, Yusuf S, Gerstein HC, et al. Circulation 2000; 102:1014.
