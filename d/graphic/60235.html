Intraosseous blood supply of the fifth metatarsal
The three principal arteries are: the metaphyseal arteries, periosteal arteries, and nutrient artery. High rates of nonunion among acute and stress fractures of the proximal diaphysis may be attributable to its relatively poor blood supply via the nutrient artery.
