Choice between MMRV and separate MMR and varicella vaccines for routine immunization in children[1]
This figure summarizes the approach to choosing between MMRV vaccine and separate MMR and varicella vaccines for routine immunization of children ≥12 months and <13 years of age. Refer to UpToDate's topic on MMR immunization in children for details.
MMRV: combination measles-mumps-rubella-varicella vaccine; MMR: combination measles-mumps-rubella vaccine.
* There is a slightly increased risk of febrile seizures 1 to 2 weeks after the first dose with MMRV compared with separate MMR and varicella vaccines (approximately 1 additional febrile seizure for every 2300 to 2800 doses of MMRV). MMRV is an alternative for caregivers who prefer to avoid an extra injection.[1-4]
¶ The risk of febrile seizures is not increased with MMRV compared with separate MMR and varicella vaccines for the second dose or among children age ≥4 years.[1,5]
