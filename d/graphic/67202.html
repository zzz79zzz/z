Genotypic and phenotypic variants of junctional epidermolysis bullosa (lucidolytic)

Genotypic and phenotypic variants of junctional epidermolysis bullosa (lucidolytic)
Subtype Inheritance
Mutated gene
Targeted gene product		 Clinical features
JEB, generalized severe (formerly Herlitz)
  • Autosomal recessive
  • LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2
  • Laminin-alpha-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-beta-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-gamma-2 chain of laminin-332
  • Varying degrees of mechanical fragility at birth with generalized, recurrent, often persistent blistering, erosions, and crusting covering not only particularly exposed skin areas (like palms and soles) but most or almost all of the body surface
  • Periungual inflammation, onychodystrophy, and nail loss
  • Exuberant granulation tissue, often periorificial
  • Involvement of oral cavity (blisters, erosions); teeth (enamel hypoplasia, excessive caries); gastrointestinal (protein-losing enteropathy, diarrhea), respiratory, and genitourinary tract; conjunctivae
  • Anemia, growth retardation, high lethality (survival past 1 year of age uncommon)
JEB, generalized intermediate (formerly non-Herlitz)
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL17A1, LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2
  • Type XVII collagen
  • Laminin-alpha-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-beta-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-gamma-2 chain of laminin-332
  • Generalized blistering from birth, atrophic scarring, milia; "male-pattern" alopecia; nail dystrophy and loss
  • Involvement of oral cavity and teeth (excessive caries, teeth loss, enamel hypoplasia); gastrointestinal, respiratory, and genitourinary tract; conjunctivae
  • Anemia, growth retardation, protein losing enteropathy, diarrhea
JEB, localized
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL17A1, ITGB4, LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2
  • Type XVII collagen
  • Integrin-beta-4
  • Laminin-alpha-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-beta-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-gamma-2 chain of laminin-332
  • Localized disease of lesser extent and lesser severity, onset at birth; milia, nail dystrophy, and loss
  • Involvement of oral cavity and teeth (excessive caries, enamel hypoplasia)
  • Extensive atrophic scarring, hair loss, anemia, impairments of growth and development, ocular abnormalities or alterations affecting gastrointestinal, genitourinary, and respiratory tract mostly absent[1]
JEB with pyloric atresia
  • Autosomal recessive
  • ITGA6, ITGB4
  • Integrin-alpha-6
  • Integrin-beta-4
  • Gestational hydramnion
  • Generalized and profound blistering from birth, occasionally age-associated amelioration; atrophic scarring; nail dystrophy and loss
  • Association with aplasia cutis congenita
  • Enamel hypoplasia
  • Pyloric atresia
  • Congenital genitourinary malformations
  • Sporadically rudimentary ear anlage
JEB, inversa
  • Autosomal recessive
  • LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2
  • Laminin-alpha-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-beta-3 chain of laminin-332
  • Laminin-gamma-2 chain of laminin-332
  • Blistering predominantly located in intertriginous areas/sites, typically displaying an overall extent that exceeds that observed in patients with JEB, localized[1]
  • Atrophic scarring, milia, hypo-/hyperpigmentation, dystrophic (or absent) nails
  • Moderate oral mucosal lesions; dental enamel hypoplasia, caries and gastrointestinal abnormalities to a lesser extent as compared with other JEB variants
JEB, late onset
  • Autosomal recessive
  • COL17A1
  • Type XVII collagen
  • Mild form of JEB developing in young adulthood or later
  • Dystrophic (or absent) nails, dental enamel hypoplasia and caries to a lesser severity than JEB, generalized intermediate
  • Hyperhidrosis
  • Absence of dermatoglyphs on fingers, palms, toes, and soles
JEB-LOC (laryngo-onycho-cutaneous) syndrome[2-11]
  • Autosomal recessive
  • LAMA3A
  • Laminin-alpha-3a chain of laminin-332
  • High incidence in Punjab
  • Minimal skin blistering, erosions
  • Extensive aberrant production of granulation tissue with delayed healing, atrophic scarring
  • Involvement of larynx (altered cry) at birth or later, skin (face, nuchal) and conjunctiva (granulomatous papules, symblepharon) in infancy
  • Nail dystrophy and loss
  • Enamel hypoplasia, caries
  • High childhood mortality due to laryngeal, gastrointestinal, and urethral strictures
JEB with respiratory and renal involvement (JEB-RR)[10-12]
  • Autosomal recessive
  • ITGA3
  • Integrin-alpha-3
  • Congenital nephrotic syndrome (small kidneys, atrophic glomeruli, focal segmental glomerulosclerosis, diffuse interstitial fibrosis, tubular atrophy and loss)
  • Interstitial lung disease (tachypnea, respiratory distress, cyanosis in first days of life; secondary aspiration with respiratory tract infections)
  • Mild skin fragility (small blisters and erosions beginning at age of 2 to 4 months
  • Fine and sparse scalp hair, eyebrows, eyelashes
  • Nail dystrophy, distal onycholysis
  • Delayed re-epithelialization; healing with residual erythema
  • No scarring; no mucosal involvement
JEB: junctional epidermolysis bullosa.
