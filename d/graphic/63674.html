Granulomatous diseases Diagnostic tests
Infections
Mycobacterial
Tuberculosis (M. tuberculosis) PPD, CXR, QFT-G, AFB smear and culture
Leprosy (M. leprae) Skin smear or biopsy, PCR
Atypical mycobacterial (eg, M. chelonei) AFB smear and culture
Treponemal
Syphilis RPR, FTA-ABS
Bacterial
Rhinoscleroma (Klebsiella rhinoscleromatis) Culture
Fungal
Histoplasmosis (Histoplasma capsulatum) Special stains, culture, serology
Protozoan
Leishmaniasis (Leishmania braziliensis) Skin smear or aspirate, PCR, biopsy showing intra and extracellular Leishman bodies
Rhinosporidiosis (Rhinosporidium seeberi) Biopsy showing granulomas and typical sporangia
Vasculitides
Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Wegener's) ANCA, biopsy
Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Churg-Strauss) Peripheral eosinophilia, ANCA, biopsy
Malignancy
Lethal midline granulomatosis (extranodal NK/T cell lymphoma) Biopsy showing polymorphous lymphoid infiltrate; immunophenotype positive for CD2, surface CD3, CD56; clonal EBV genomes present
Nonkeratinizing nasopharyngeal carcinoma Biopsy shows an epithelial neoplasm with a dense lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate (predominantly T cells); associated with EBV infection
Other
Sarcoidosis Biopsy showing noncaseating granulomas, CXR
Cocaine-induced midline destructive lesion History of cocaine use
Granulomatous rosacea Granulomatous perifolliculitis
PPD: purified protein derivative skin test; CXR: chest radiograph; QFT-G: Quantiferon-Gold; AFB: acid fast bacilli; PCR: polymerase chain reaction; RPR: rapid plasma reagin; FTA-ABS: fluorescent treponemal antibody absorption; ANCA: antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibody; EBV: Epstein Barr virus.
Data from: Zargari O, Elpern DJ. Granulomatous diseases of the nose. Int J Dermatol 2009; 48:1275.
