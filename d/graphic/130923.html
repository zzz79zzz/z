Chances that a complete placenta previa diagnosed antepartum will be present at delivery based on most recent ultrasound examination
These data were derived from a study[1] of 714 women between 15 and 36 weeks of gestation with antepartum ultrasound evidence of placenta previa. All were singleton gestations and delivered a live born infant at ≥25 weeks of gestation.
CD: cesarean delivery.
Reference:
  1. Dashe JS, McIntire DD, Ramus RM, et al. Persistence of placenta previa according to gestational age at ultrasound detection. Obstet Gynecol 2002; 99:692.
