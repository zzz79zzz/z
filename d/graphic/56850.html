Incidence of Campylobacter enteritis in the United States, by age
Reported campylobacter isolations by age and sex in the United States from 1982 to 1986. There is a high incidence in early childhood (with a peak in the first three months) and a pronounced secondary peak in young adults, a feature not seen with salmonella or shigella infection.
Redrawn from Tauxe RV. Epidemiology of Campylobacter enteritis in the United States, American Society for Microbiology, Washington, DC, 1992, p. 9.
