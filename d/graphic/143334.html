Comorbid conditions that may worsen asthma control
Comorbidity Potential interventions
Obesity
  • Counseling regarding nutrition and physical activity
  • Referral for specialized weight loss management
Smoking
  • Counseling regarding smoking cessation
  • Pharmacologic aids for those interested in quitting
  • Referral to smoking cessation programs and counseling
Obstructive sleep apnea
  • Any treatment to prevent apneic episodes (eg, continuous positive airway pressure devices, mandibular advancement devices) may improve nocturnal dyspnea and choking sensations
Allergic rhinitis
  • Allergen avoidance
  • Intranasal glucocorticoids
  • Antihistamines ± decongestants ± anti-leukotrienes
  • Allergen immunotherapy
Chronic rhinosinusitis (particularly with nasal polyposis)
  • Intranasal glucocorticoids
  • Antimicrobial agents, in some patients
  • Aspirin desensitization, in some patients
  • Otolaryngology referral for consideration of surgical intervention
Gastroesophageal reflux disease
  • Treatment of symptomatic disease with acid-suppressing agents
Inducible laryngeal obstruction
  • Referral to otolaryngology for diagnosis and speech therapy for treatment
Anxiety and depression
  • Counseling and/or pharmacologic management
Common comorbid conditions that may negatively impact asthma control, accompanied by possible mitigation strategies. Please refer to additional UpToDate content on the evaluation and management of these comorbid conditions for additional details.
