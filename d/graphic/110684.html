Fish-hook removal: Cut it out technique
Only use this technique as a last resort. For other techniques, refer to UpToDate topics on fish-hook removal. Anesthetize the skin at the entrance site. Make an incision with a scalpel along the body of the hook to the barb. For deeply embedded hooks, use a hemostat to bluntly dissect to the barb once the skin incision is made. Remove the hook along the path of the incision.
