Capnogram with upsloping plateau
In patients with obstructive lung disease and an upsloping ETCO2 plateau, inspiration may occur before the true end of expiration, and result in a falsely low ETCO2 (solid line). Reducing the respiratory rate (or briefly stopping the ventilator) should allow full exhalation and an accurate ETCO2 reading (dashed line).
CO2: carbon dioxide; ETCO2: end-tidal carbon dioxide.
