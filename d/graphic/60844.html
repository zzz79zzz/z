Brain MRI in cerebral autosomal dominant arteriopathy with subcortical infarcts and leukoencephalopathy (CADASIL)
Axial images of the brain with fluid-attenuated inversion-recovery (FLAIR) MRI in representative patients with biopsy-proved CADASIL show a typical pattern of brain lesions. Note the symmetry of the lesions and their extension into the superficial white matter (arrows).
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
