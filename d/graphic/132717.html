Hutchinson-Gilford progeria syndrome
Ten-year-old White male (A) and 5-year-old White female (B) with typical Hutchinson-Gilford progeria syndrome facial features (ie, loss of subcutaneous fat, beaked nose, missing eyelashes and eyebrows, alopecia with easy mapping of the scalp veins, absence of earlobes, and micrognathia).
From: Domingo DL, Trujillo MI, Council SE, et al. Hutchinson-Gilford progeria syndrome: oral and craniofacial phenotypes.
