Ventricular aneurysm
Electrocardiogram showing anterior wall myocardial infarction with probable ventricular aneurysm. Persistent ST segment elevation in leads V1 to V4 three or more weeks after a Q-wave myocardial infarction is a relatively specific but not highly sensitive sign of severe ventricular motion abnormality and sometimes frank aneurysm formation. Note also that the rhythm is sinus. There is borderline left axis deviation (about -30°) with an intraventricular conduction delay.
