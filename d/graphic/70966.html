One-way sensitivity analysis (example for decision analysis)
One-way sensitivity analysis of the probability of a complication from thrombolytic therapy. As the likelihood of a complication (x-axis) rises, the expected value of the thrombolysis strategy declines. The point at which the probability of survival is equivalent between the two strategies is called the threshold.
Data from: Krumholz HM, Pasternak RC, Weinstein MC, et al. N Engl J Med 1992; 327:7.
