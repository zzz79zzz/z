Knee osteoarthritis
This drawing shows a normal knee joint next to a knee joint with osteoarthritis. In the osteoarthritis joint, the cartilage covering the ends of the bones roughens and becomes thin, while the bone underneath the cartilage grows thicker. Bony growths called "osteophytes" can form. The space between the bones also becomes narrower.
