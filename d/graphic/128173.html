Neuroimaging in preterm infant with Grade 3 CBH through term equivalent
Neuroimaging of preterm infant (gestational age 25 weeks) with grade 3 cerebellar hemorrhage (CBH) detected by ultrasound at fourth postnatal day with follow-up by magnetic resonance imagine (MRI) at term equivalent demonstrating the chronic finding of atrophy.
(Image A) Cranial ultrasound on 4th postnatal day, left mastoid fontanel view, demonstrating CBH as increased echogenicity in right cerebellar hemisphere (arrow).
(Image B and C) MRI at term equivalent age in the same preterm infant demonstrating the chronic atrophic stage of CBH with (B) T2-weighted showing an asymmetry and atrophy of the right cerebellar hemisphere (arrowhead) and (C) susceptibility-weighted image with hypointense signal in the right cerebellar hemisphere (thick arrow) confirming this is not unilateral cerebellar hypoplasia but atrophy in the chronic stage of CBH.
