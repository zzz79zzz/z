Differential diagnoses for maternal early warning criteria

Differential diagnoses for maternal early warning criteria
Blood pressure >160 systolic or >100 diastolic
Common diagnoses
  • Chronic hypertension
  • Preeclampsia
  • Sympathomimetic medications (methylergonovine, phenylephrine, other vasopressors)

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Illicit substances (eg, cocaine, amphetamines)
  • Endocrine disorders (pheochromocytoma, hyperthyroidism, renal artery stenosis)
  • Hypoventilation with hypercarbia (eg, opiate-related, obstructive sleep apnea, respiratory muscle weakness)
Blood pressure <90 systolic
Common diagnoses
  • Dehydration
  • Supine hypotension syndrome
  • Regional anesthesia side-effect
    • Hypotension solely due to regional anesthesia is unusual if the cephalad anesthetic level is at T10 or below
  • Hemorrhage
  • Medication side-effects (beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers)
  • Vasovagal reaction (in presence of bradycardia)

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Sepsis
  • Cardiac (cardiomyopathy, right or left heart failure, myocardial ischemia, pericarditis, cardiac tamponade, arrhythmia)
  • Thromboembolism (PE)
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Drug toxicity (magnesium, local anesthetic)
  • High spinal or epidural block
  • Tension pneumothorax
  • Vascular emergency (splanchnic arterial dissection, aortic dissection)
Heart rate >120 bpm
Common diagnoses
  • Dehydration
  • Hemorrhage
  • Infection
  • Medication side effects (sympathomimetics, beta-2 agonists)
  • Anxiety/pain/panic attack

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Cardiac (cardiomyopathy, right or left heart failure, myocardial ischemia, pericarditis, pericardial tamponade, tachyarrhythmia)
  • Illicit drugs (eg, cocaine, amphetamines)
  • Thromboembolism (PE)
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Endocrine disorders (pheochromocytoma, hyperthyroidism/thyroid storm)
  • Malignant hyperthermia
  • Vascular emergency (splanchnic arterial dissection, aortic dissection)
Heart rate <50
Common diagnoses
  • Vasovagal reaction (in presence of hypotension)
  • Medication side-effects (beta-blockers)
  • Hypertension (reflex bradycardia)

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Heart block from cardiac disease (eg, myocardial infarction, myocarditis, or hemochromatosis)
  • Hypoventilation or hypoxemia (eg, obstructive sleep apnea)
  • Electrolyte disturbances (K, Na, Ca, Mg)
  • Medication toxicity (magnesium)
  • Stroke/increased intracranial pressure
  • High spinal or epidural block
Respiratory rate >30
Common diagnoses
  • Hypoxia (eg, asthma)
  • Hemorrhage
  • Infection (eg, sepsis, pneumonia)
  • Anxiety/panic attack/exertion

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Cardiac (cardiomyopathy, right or left heart failure, myocardial ischemia)
  • Thromboembolism (PE)
  • Aspiration
  • Metabolic acidosis including diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • Stroke
Respiratory rate <10
Common diagnoses
  • Medication toxicity (opioids, benzodiazepines, magnesium)
  • Obstructive sleep apnea

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Airway obstruction
  • Asthma with respiratory failure
  • High spinal or epidural block
  • Cerebrovascular accident
Oxygen saturation <95% or increasing oxygen requirements to maintain oxygen saturation ≥95%
Common diagnoses
  • Atelectasis (following anesthesia, obesity, supine position at the end of pregnancy)
  • Bronchospasm (asthma)
  • Hemorrhage
  • Hypoventilation (obstructive sleep apnea, opiate or benzodiazepine effect)
  • Infection (eg, pneumonia)

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Airway obstruction (asphyxia, laryngospasm)
  • Pulmonary edema
  • Thromboembolic disease (PE)
  • Cardiac disease (cardiomyopathy, right or left heart failure, intracardiac shunt)
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Aspiration
  • Acute chest syndrome (sickle cell disease)
  • Pneumothorax
  • Venous air embolism
  • Blood product reaction (TACO, TRALI, anaphylaxis)
Maternal confusion, agitation, or unresponsiveness
Common diagnoses
  • Hypoxemia
  • Hemorrhage/hypotension
  • Illicit substances (either intoxication or withdrawal)
  • Seizure/postictal state/posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (PRES)
  • Psychosis is a diagnosis of exclusion

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis, hypoglycemia
  • Sepsis
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Thromboembolic disease (PE)
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • Anesthesia complications
  • Cardiomyopathy
  • Cerebrovascular accident, cavernous sinus thrombosis
  • Increased intracranial pressure
  • Electrolyte abnormality (hypermagnesemia)
  • Hyperthyroidism/thyroid storm, myxedema coma
  • Medication toxicity
Oliguria <35 mL/hour over a two-hour period
Common diagnoses
  • Severe dehydration
  • Hemorrhage
  • Obstructed urinary catheter

Rare but life-threatening diagnoses
  • Surgical complications
  • Genitourinary tract injury
  • Preeclampsia with severe features
  • Sepsis
  • Concealed hemorrhage (retroperitoneal hemorrhage, abruption, uterine rupture)
  • Acute renal failure
PE: pulmonary embolism; bpm: beats per minute; TACO: transfusion-associated circulatory overload; TRALI: transfusion-related acute lung injury.
