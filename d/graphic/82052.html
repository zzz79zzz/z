Electrophysiology (EP) study tracing obtained during radiofrequency catheter ablation of atrial tachycardia
Three surface ECG leads (I, aVF, V1) and intracardiac recordings from the high posterior right atrium (HRA); posterior left atrium (USER1 and USER 3); proximal, mid, and distal coronary sinus (CS9-10, CS5-6, CS1-2); and right ventricular apex (RVA) in a patient with atrial tachycardia. Radiofrequency energy application at the left atrial site of earliest atrial activation (USER1) causes initial rate acceleration, consistent with heating of an automatic focus; this is followed by abrupt termination (*) of the atrial tachycardia, resulting in a junctional rhythm. The patient had no further tachycardia during follow-up and left ventricular function returned to normal within four months of the ablation procedure, confirming that the cardiomyopathy was tachycardia induced.
