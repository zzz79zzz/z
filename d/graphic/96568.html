Rheumatoid arthritis hand deformities: Boutonniere deformity and Z-deformity of the thumb
A woman with longstanding rheumatoid arthritis has soft tissue swelling and subluxation of the metacarpophalangeal joints. The right thumb shows hyperextension of the interphalangeal joint (a Z deformity). Both ring fingers have boutonniere deformities with flexion of the proximal and hyperextension of the distal interphalangeal joints.
