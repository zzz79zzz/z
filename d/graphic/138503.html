The Cancer-Immunity Cycle
The generation of immunity to cancer is a cyclic process that can be self-propagating and cumulative with amplification and broadening of the T cell response. There are also inhibitory factors (not shown) that lead to regulatory feedback mechanisms that can curtail the immune response.
Prepared with data from: Chen DS, Mellman I. Oncology meets immunology: the cancer-immunity cycle. Immunity 2013; 39:1.
