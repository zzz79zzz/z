Esophageal contractile response patterns on FLIP panometry[1,2]
The definitions of contractile response in the esophagus are based on studies that used the 16 cm catheter and assessed the contractile response to distension using FLIP data ranging from the 50 to 70 mL fill volumes.
FLIP: functional lumen imaging probe; EGJ: esophagogastric junction.
References:
  1. Carlson DA, Baumann AJ, Donnan EN, et al. Evaluating esophageal motility beyond primary peristalsis: Assessing esophagogastric junction opening mechanics and secondary peristalsis in patients with normal manometry. Neurogastroenterol Motil 2021; 33:e14116.
  2. Carlson DA, Baumann AJ, Prescott JE, et al. Validation of secondary peristalsis classification using FLIP panometry in 741 subjects undergoing manometry. Neurogastroenterol Motil 2021; 34:e14192.
