Warning
  • urn:lims:b498:s6808720 • No Rarely, this drug may cause low blood cell counts or other severe blood problems like thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura (TTP) and aplastic anemia. This may be life-threatening. Most of the time, this happened 3 to 4 weeks after starting this drug. However, rarely this has happened the day of starting this drug or more than 12 weeks after starting it. Have your blood work checked as you have been told by your doctor. Call your doctor right away if you feel confused, dizzy, or very tired. Call your doctor right away if you have mood changes; trouble speaking; numbness, tingling, weakness, or trouble moving around; fever; change in how much urine is passed; red or purple spots on the skin; seizures; or signs of liver problems like dark urine, light-colored stools, or yellow skin or eyes.
