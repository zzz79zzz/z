Aromatic ammonia spirit: Drug information

Pharmacologic Category
  • Respiratory Stimulant
Dosing: Adult
Fainting

Fainting: Inhalation: Slowly inhale the vapors of one crushed ampul or opened bottle of solution until fainting resolves.

Dosing: Older Adult

Refer to adult dosing.

Dosing: Pediatric
Fainting

Fainting: Children >12 years: Inhalation: Refer to adult dosing.

Adverse Reactions

There are no adverse reactions listed in the manufacturer's labeling.

Postmarketing: Hypersensitivity: Hypersensitivity reaction (Herrick 1983)

Warnings/Precautions

Concerns related to adverse effects:

• Fainting: Patients should be evaluated for the cause of fainting, especially when accompanied by flushed face.

Disease-related concerns:

• Ocular disease: Use with caution in patients with diseases of the eye; conditions may worsen.

• Respiratory disease: Use with caution in patients with asthma, bronchitis, emphysema, or other chronic lung conditions.

Dosage Forms: US

Excipient information presented when available (limited, particularly for generics); consult specific product labeling.

Solution, for vapor inhalation: 2% (60 mL [DSC])

Generic Equivalent Available: US

Yes

Administration: Adult

The vapor of aromatic ammonia spirit may be inhaled; keep away from eyes and skin. Crush one ampul between fingers or open container of solution; slowly inhale when ampule or solution is held ~4 inches away from nostrils.

Administration: Pediatric

Inhalation: The vapor of aromatic ammonia spirit may be inhaled; keep away from eyes and skin. Crush one ampul between fingers or open container of solution; slowly inhale when ampule or solution is held ~4 inches away from nostrils.

Use: Labeled Indications

Prevention or treatment of fainting

Metabolism/Transport Effects

None known.

Drug Interactions

There are no known significant interactions.
