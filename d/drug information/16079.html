What are some things I need to know or do while I take this drug?
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697633 • No Tell all of your health care providers that you take this drug. This includes your doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and dentists.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2858939 • No Avoid driving and doing other tasks or actions that call for you to be alert until you see how this drug affects you.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5984616 • No Slow heartbeat and a heartbeat that does not feel normal may happen when first starting this drug. Most of the time, this happens within 6 hours of your first dose. Sometimes, this can happen up to 24 hours after your first dose. Call your doctor right away if you have dizziness, slow heartbeat or a heartbeat that does not feel normal, or tiredness.
  • urn:lims:b498:s7254899 • No This drug stays in the body and may affect your immune system for up to 2 months after your last dose. If you will be taking another drug that will suppress your immune system within 2 months after you stop this drug, talk with your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5150272 • No You may have more chance of getting an infection while you take this drug and for 2 months after you stop it. Some infections have been severe and even deadly. Wash hands often. Stay away from people with infections, colds, or flu.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5150274 • No Talk with your doctor before getting any vaccines while you take this drug and for 2 months after you stop it. Use with this drug may either raise the chance of an infection or make the vaccine not work as well.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6918108 • No HPV infections have happened with this drug. This includes HPV-related growths and cancer. You may need to have an HPV vaccine before taking this drug. Have Pap smears as you have been told by your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6724202 • No When this drug is stopped, MS signs can come back and may be worse than before or during treatment. Many people do not go back to the same level of function they had before this drug was stopped. Most of the time, this effect happened in the first 12 weeks after stopping this drug but can happen later. If you stop this drug and your MS signs get worse, call your doctor right away.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5529215 • No This drug may raise the chance of getting cancer like lymphoma or skin cancer. Call your doctor right away if you have a change in color or size of a mole, a skin lump or growth, a big weight loss, night sweats, or swollen glands.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697657 • No The chance of skin cancer may be raised. Avoid lots of sun, sunlamps, and tanning beds. Use sunscreen and wear clothing and eyewear that protects you from the sun.
  • urn:lims:b498:s5552080 • No Have your skin checked. Tell your doctor if you have any skin changes like a new wart, skin sore or reddish bump that bleeds or does not heal, or a change in the color or size of a mole.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6319200 • No Liver problems have happened with this drug. Sometimes, this has been very bad and a liver transplant has been needed. Talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697636 • Female No Tell your doctor if you are breast-feeding. You will need to talk about any risks to your baby.
