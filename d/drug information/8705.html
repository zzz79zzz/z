Pharmacologic Category
  • Antiemetic
Dosing: Adult
Nausea

Nausea: Oral: 15 to 30 mL; repeat dose every 15 minutes until distress subsides; do not take >5 doses in 1 hour.

Dosing: Kidney Impairment: Adult

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer's labeling.

Dosing: Hepatic Impairment: Adult

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer's labeling.

Dosing: Older Adult

Refer to adult dosing.

Dosing: Pediatric
Nausea

Nausea: Oral

Children 2 to 12 years: 5 to 10 mL; repeat dose every 15 minutes until distress subsides; do not take for more than 1 hour (5 doses)

Children ≥12 years: Refer to adult dosing.

Adverse Reactions

There are no adverse reactions listed in the manufacturer's labeling. See Dextrose monograph.

Contraindications

OTC labeling: When used for self-medication, do not use if you have ever had an allergic reaction to fructose, dextrose, phosphoric acid, or any component of the formulation, or if you have hereditary fructose intolerance.

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

• Self-medication (OTC use): When used for self-medication (OTC), check with health care provider before use if you have diabetes. Notify health care provider if symptoms persist, return, or worsen, or if you need >5 doses in 1 hour.

Administration: Adult

Oral: Should not be diluted or followed with other fluids immediately before or after administration. Use dosing cup (if provided) or an accurate measuring device (if one is not provided) to measure doses.

Administration: Pediatric

Oral: Do not dilute or drink other fluids immediately before or after administration.

Use: Labeled Indications

Nausea: Relief of nausea associated with upset stomach that occurs with intestinal or stomach flu, and food indiscretions.

Metabolism/Transport Effects
