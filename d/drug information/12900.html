Dosing: Pediatric
Constipation

Constipation: Oral: OTC tablet (Docusate 50 mg and sennosides 8.6 mg/tablet):

Children 2 to <6 years: Initial: 1/2 tablet (docusate 25 mg/sennosides 4.3 mg) once daily at bedtime; may increase to maximum dose of 1 tablet (docusate 50 mg/sennosides 8.6 mg) twice daily

Children 6 to <12 years: Initial: 1 tablet (docusate 50 mg/sennosides 8.6 mg) once daily at bedtime; may increase to a maximum dose of: 2 tablets (docusate 100 mg/sennosides 17.2 mg) twice daily

Children ≥12 years and Adolescents: Initial: 2 tablets (docusate 100 mg/sennosides 17.2 mg) once daily at bedtime; may increase to a maximum dose of: 4 tablets (docusate 200 mg/sennosides 34.4 mg) twice daily

Dosing: Adult

(For additional information see "Docusate and senna: Drug information")

Constipation

Constipation: Oral: Initial: 2 tablets (17.2 mg sennosides plus 100 mg docusate) once daily (maximum: 4 tablets twice daily).

Adverse Reactions

The following adverse drug reactions and incidences are derived from product labeling unless otherwise specified. Frequency not defined.

Gastrointestinal: Abdominal cramps, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting

Genitourinary: Urine discoloration (red/brown)

Contraindications

OTC labeling: When used for self-medication, do not use for >7 days or concomitantly with mineral oil.

Significant drug interactions exist, requiring dose/frequency adjustment or avoidance. Consult drug interactions database for more information.

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

• OTC labeling: Not recommended for over-the-counter (OTC) use in patients experiencing stomach pain, nausea, vomiting, or a sudden change in bowel movements which lasts >2 weeks. Not recommended for use longer than 1 week. Not recommended for use in children <2 years of age. Discontinue use and contact healthcare provider if rectal bleeding occurs.

Oral: Administer with water at bedtime

Administration: Adult

Oral: Once-daily doses should be taken at bedtime.

Use

Relief of occasional constipation (OTC products; FDA approved in ages ≥2 years and adults; consult specific product formulations for appropriate age groups)

Medication Safety Issues
Sound-alike/look-alike issues:

Senokot may be confused with Depakote

Dietary Considerations

Some products may contain sodium.

Pregnancy Considerations

See individual agents.

Monitoring Parameters

Fluid status, frequency of bowel movements, serum electrolytes if severe diarrhea occurs

Mechanism of Action

Docusate is a stool softener; sennosides are laxatives
