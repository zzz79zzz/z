  • urn:lims:b498:s4470402 • No Do not take this drug for longer than you were told by your doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4194396 • No This drug may affect certain lab tests. Tell all of your health care providers and lab workers that you take this drug.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4667356 • No This drug may raise the chance of hip, spine, and wrist fractures in people with weak bones (osteoporosis). The chance may be higher if you take this drug in high doses or for longer than a year, or if you are older than 50 years old.
  • urn:lims:b498:s2697754 • No Use care if you have risks for soft, brittle bones (osteoporosis). Talk with your doctor about your risks of osteoporosis.
  • urn:lims:b498:s4667365 • No Rarely, low magnesium levels have happened in people taking drugs like this one for at least 3 months. Most of the time, this happened after 1 year of treatment.   • urn:lims:b498:s6698682 • No Rarely, long-term treatment (for instance longer than 3 years) with drugs like this one has caused low vitamin B-12 levels. Call your doctor right away if you have signs of low vitamin B-12 levels like shortness of breath, dizziness, abnormal heartbeat, muscle weakness, pale skin, tiredness, mood changes, or numbness or tingling in the arms or legs.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6302711 • No Lupus has happened with this drug, as well as lupus that has gotten worse in people who already have it. Tell your doctor if you have lupus.   • urn:lims:b498:s7364859 • No The risk of stomach growths called fundic gland polyps may be higher in people who take this drug for more than 1 year. If you have questions, talk with the doctor.
  • urn:lims:b498:s6769396 • Female No Tell your doctor if you are pregnant, plan on getting pregnant, or are breast-feeding. You will need to talk about the benefits and risks to you and the baby.
