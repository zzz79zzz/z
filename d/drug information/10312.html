Adverse Reactions

The following adverse drug reactions are derived from product labeling unless otherwise specified. Adverse effects are reported for the individual agents rather than for the combination product.

Frequency not defined:

Cardiovascular: Thrombosis (peripheral)

Dermatologic: Pruritus, transient skin rash

Gastrointestinal: Diarrhea

Hematologic & oncologic: Polycythemia vera

Hypersensitivity: Anaphylactic shock

Local: Pain at injection

Miscellaneous: Swelling

Postmarketing:

Dermatologic: Acne vulgaris, dermatological reaction, skin photosensitivity

Gastrointestinal: Abdominal pain, anorexia, nausea, vomiting

Hepatic: Increased liver enzymes

Hypersensitivity: Allergic sensitization, hypersensitivity reaction

Nervous system: Drowsiness, headache, paresthesia

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to any component of the formulation.

Warnings/Precautions

Concerns related to adverse effects:

• Iron toxicity: For products containing iron: Severe iron toxicity may occur in overdose, particularly when ingested by children; iron is a leading cause of fatal poisoning in children; store out of children's reach and in child-resistant containers. Adult preparations may contain amounts of iron which should not be used in children.

Disease-related concerns:

• Pernicious anemia: For products containing folic acid: Doses of folic acid >0.1 mg/day may obscure pernicious anemia with continuing irreversible nerve damage progression.

Metabolism/Transport Effects

Drug Interactions

There are no known significant interactions.

Food Interactions

Iron absorption is inhibited by eggs and milk.

Pregnancy Considerations

Water soluble vitamins cross the placenta (IOM 1998). Refer to individual vitamins for additional information and specific requirements during pregnancy.

Breastfeeding Considerations

Refer to individual vitamins for additional information and specific requirements in nursing women.

Dietary Considerations

May be taken with food to decrease stomach upset.
