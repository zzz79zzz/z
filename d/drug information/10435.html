Pharmacologic Category
  • Antibiotic, Miscellaneous
Reported Use

Treatment of infections caused by susceptible gram-positive bacteria, including bacteremia, complicated skin and soft tissue infections, complicated urinary tract infections, community and hospital acquired pneumonia, joint and bone infections, endocarditis; C. difficile-associated diarrhea (oral only); surgical prophylaxis

Dosage Range

C. difficile-associated infections: Oral: Adults: 100 to 200 mg twice daily.

Gram-positive infections: IV/IM:

Neonates and Infants <2 months: IV: Initial: 16 mg/kg loading dose on day 1, followed by 8 mg/kg once daily starting on day 2.

Infants â¥2 months and Children â¤12 years: IV: Initial: 10 mg/kg every 12 hours for 3 doses, followed by 6 to 10 mg/kg once daily.

Adolescents and Adults: IV: 6 to 12 mg/kg every 12 hours for 3 to 5 doses. Maintenance: IV or IM: 6 to 12 mg/kg once daily to achieve targeted trough concentration.

Surgical (orthopedic) prophylaxis: IV: 400 mg (or 6 mg/kg) given once at induction of anesthesia.

Product Availability

Product available in various countries; not currently available in the US

Dosage Forms

Solution Reconstituted, Injection/Oral: 200 mg, 400 mg
