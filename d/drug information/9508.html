Brand Names: US

Pharmacologic Category
  • Diuretic
Dosing: Adult
Premenstrual or menstrual symptoms

Premenstrual or menstrual symptoms: Oral: 50 mg after breakfast, may repeat in 6 hours as needed (maximum: 200 mg/24 hours); should be taken 5 to 6 days prior to onset of menstrual period and continued until desired relief or end of period.

Adverse Reactions

There are no adverse reactions listed in the manufacturer's labeling.

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

• Self-medication (OTC use): Patients should drink 6 to 8 glasses of water daily while taking. Patients should be instructed to contact healthcare provider if new symptoms occur or if symptoms last >10 consecutive days.

• Urine discoloration: May temporarily cause urine to be golden tinted.

Dosage Forms: US

Excipient information presented when available (limited, particularly for generics); consult specific product labeling.

Tablet, Oral:

Diurex Max: 50 mg

Generic Equivalent Available: US

No

Administration: Adult

Administer with a full glass of water. Patients should be instructed to drink 6 to 8 glasses of water/day while taking pamabrom.

Use: Labeled Indications

Temporary relief of symptoms associated with premenstrual and menstrual periods (eg, bloating, water-weight gain, swelling, full feeling)

Metabolism/Transport Effects

None known.

Drug Interactions

There are no known significant interactions.
