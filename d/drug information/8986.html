Dosing: Adult
Antacid

Antacid: Oral:

Chewable tablet: Calcium carbonate 700 mg/magnesium hydroxide 300 mg: Chew 2 to 4 tablets 4 times daily (maximum: 8 tablets per 24 hours).

Dosage adjustment for concomitant therapy: Significant drug interactions exist, requiring dose/frequency adjustment or avoidance. Consult drug interactions database for more information.

Dosing: Kidney Impairment: Adult

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling; magnesium may accumulate in renal impairment.

There are no dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling.

Dosing: Older Adult

Refer to adult dosing.

Adverse Reactions

See Calcium Carbonate monograph.

Warnings/Precautions

Dosage form specific issues:

• Benzyl alcohol and derivatives: Some dosage forms may contain benzyl alcohol; large amounts of benzyl alcohol (≥99 mg/kg/day) have been associated with a potentially fatal toxicity (“gasping syndrome”) in neonates; the “gasping syndrome” consists of metabolic acidosis, respiratory distress, gasping respirations, CNS dysfunction (including convulsions, intracranial hemorrhage), hypotension, and cardiovascular collapse (AAP 1997; CDC 1982); avoid or use dosage forms containing benzyl alcohol with caution in neonates. See manufacturer’s labeling.

Other warnings/precautions:

• Self-medication (OTC use): When used for self-medication, patients should be instructed to consult their health care prescriber prior to using if they have renal impairment or if they are on a magnesium restricted diet. Do not take the maximum dose for >14 days.

Administration: Adult

Administer after meals and at bedtime (4 times daily). Shake liquid well before use. Do not swallow chewable tablets whole.

Use: Labeled Indications

Antacid: Relief of heartburn, acid indigestion, sour stomach, and GI upset associated with these symptoms; relief of belching, bloating, abdominal pressure/discomfort associated with extra gas.

Pregnancy Considerations

Calcium-based antacids are considered low risk during pregnancy; excessive use should be avoided (Mahadevan 2006). Most magnesium-containing antacids are considered low risk during pregnancy (Mahadevan 2006).

Breastfeeding Considerations

Calcium-based antacids are probably compatible with breast-feeding (Mahadevan 2006). Most magnesium-containing antacids are considered low risk in nursing women (Mahadevan 2006).

Dietary Considerations

Should be taken after meals.
